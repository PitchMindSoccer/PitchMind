**Content Overview:**
- Striker role as focal point + work rate contributor
- Five fundamental components (movement, hold-up, pressing, finishing, link play)
- Modern striker expectations (complete forward, not just finisher)
- Position in 4-3-3 formation (60+ yards from own goal, advanced)
- Integration with midfield and widths

**Key Frameworks:**
1. **Movement > Speed** (timing run correctly beats running faster)
2. **Hold-up is positional** (back to defender beats strength)
3. **Pressing is trigger-based** (press center-back when they receive)
4. **Position > Technique** (good position beats technical skill)
5. **Link play creates consistency** (creating for teammates beats only scoring)

**Component 1: Movement (5 Types Detailed)**
- Away runs (create space, check back)
- Across runs (use width, penetrate)
- Blind runs (timing-based combinations)
- Deep runs (transition to midfield)
- Through-ball runs (explosive into space)

**Component 2: Hold-Up Play (Reception Mastery)**
- Receiving position (back 45° to defender)
- First touch (soft, sideways, creating separation)
- Awareness (know where pass goes pre-receiving)
- Release (one-touch to teammate run)
- Progression: unopposed → light pressure → active → match

**Component 3: Pressing from Front**
- Trigger recognition (when center-back has ball)
- Approach technique (explosive sprint, position between ball and goal)
- Pressure maintenance (block forward pass)
- Recovery discipline (don't chase after bypass)
- Position-specific trigger (different from CDM/fullback)

**Component 4: Finishing Runs (5 Types)**
- Near post run (fastest option, goalkeeper can't reach)
- Far post run (timing-based header opportunity)
- Middle finish (1v1 vs. goalkeeper, most common)
- Tap-in (goalkeeper rebound, easy finish)
- Turning finish (receive back-to-goal, turn and shoot)

**Component 5: Link Play (5 Patterns)**
- One-two (simplest combination)
- Flick-pass (advanced, side-foot technique)
- Hold + release (timing-dependent)
- Layoff pass (quick progression)
- Through-ball creation (advanced)

**Teaching Progression (4 Levels):**
1. Movement & receiving (unopposed)
2. Hold-up & link play (light pressure)

### ✅ FILE 3: CDM (DEFENSIVE MIDFIELDER) FUNDAMENTALS
**File:** `positions/cdm-fundamentals.md` | **Size:** 27.9 KB

**Content Overview:**
- CDM as structural anchor (pivot role, coverage responsibility)
- Five fundamental components (pivot, screening, distribution, pressing, transition)
- Role variation (pure pivot vs. pressing vs. ball-progressive)
- Positioning depth framework (25-45 yards from own goal varies by situation)
- Critical coverage function (enables fullbacks/box-to-box aggression)

**Key Frameworks:**
1. **Position > Technique** (anticipation beats technical skill)
2. **Coverage > Pressing** (preventing opportunities beats making plays)
3. **Distribution under pressure (learned skill, specific training)
4. **Pressing is selective** (cover more than press, triggers infrequent)
5. **Transition tempo = match intelligence** (fast when space exists, slow when organized)

**Component 1: Pivot Role (Structural Positioning)**
- Deep pivot (25-30 yards) = defensive heavy, fullbacks aggressive
- Mid-pivot (35-40 yards) = balanced, standard positioning
- Advanced pivot (40-45 yards) = attack heavy, fullbacks deep
- Decision tree based on opposition threat + team possession
- Depth adjustment training (unopposed → match-realistic)

**Component 2: Screening Backline (Protection Function)**
- Blocking passing lanes (positional defense, not tackling)
- Pressure recognition (identify where opposition will penetrate)
- Body positioning (between ball-carrier and danger)
- Communication (vocal organization, call out coverage)
- Progression: unopposed → light opposition → active opposition → match

**Component 3: Distribution Under Pressure (Technical + Decision)**
- Recognize pressure early (anticipate before pressure arrives)
- Identify exit pass (know where ball goes before receiving)
- Execute exit pass (quick delivery, accuracy)
- Decision framework (space = creative, light pressure = forward, heavy = backward)
- Risk management (backward pass is safest under heavy pressure)

**Component 4: Pressing (Selective, Coverage Priority)**
- Low pressing frequency (3-5 times per match, not 10+)
- Trigger 1: Low-risk press (cleanup press when others already pressed)
- Trigger 2: Coverage press (block dangerous forward pass)
- Trigger 3: Never press (standard role, others press, CDM covers)
- Discipline over aggression (staying back > pressing indiscriminately)

**Component 5: Transitional Responsibility (Tempo Setting)**
- Fast transition (forward pass when space exists)
- Moderate transition (sideways then forward)
- Slow transition (backward/sideways recycle)
- Decision framework (opposition shape determines tempo)
- Timing drill progression (unopposed → light → active → match)

**Teaching Progression (4 Levels):**
1. Positioning & recognition (unopposed, shape understanding)
2. Distribution & decision-making (light pressure, safe options)
3. Pressing & coverage integration (semi-opposed)
4. Match-realistic 11v11 (all components integrated)

**Application:**
- Structural development (CDM enables team defense)
- Coverage mastery (preventing penetration)
- Decision-making speed (distribution under pressure)
- Transition tempo control (counter-attack initiation)

### ✅ FILE 5: PRESSING SYSTEMS ADVANCED
**File:** `coaching/pressing-systems-advanced.md` | **Size:** 33.1 KB

**Content Overview:**
- Five pressing systems (high press, mid-block, deep block, traps, gegenpressing)
- Decision framework (when to use which system)
- Position-specific pressing triggers
- Energy management (sustainability, pressing windows)
- Opposition analysis (determine pressing level by opponent)

**Key Frameworks:**
1. **Pressing trigger > intensity** (correct timing beats hard pressure)
2. **Organization > effort** (coordinated pressing beats individual)
3. **Position determines responsibility** (each position presses different trigger)
4. **Recovery system prevents penetration** (failed press → organized reset)
5. **Opposition-dependent system** (pressing adapted to opponent strength)

**System 1: HIGH PRESS (Aggressive, Near Opposition Goal)**
- **Depth:** 40-60 yards from own goal
- **When:** vs. weak technical opposition, direct-play teams, leading position
- **Duration:** 15-30 minutes per match (not entire match)
- **Positions:** Striker 60+, fullbacks 50-55, box-to-box 45-50, pivot 35-40 (deepest)
- **Advantages:** Ball recovery near opposition goal, forces errors, opponent discomfort
- **Disadvantages:** High energy, risk of through-ball, ineffective vs. possession teams
- **Recovery:** Reset to mid-block if high press fails

**System 2: MID-BLOCK (Balanced, 55-60 Yard Line)**
- **Depth:** 55-60 yards from own goal
- **When:** vs. balanced opposition, match is level, sustainable pressing
- **Duration:** 45-90 minutes (entire match capable)
- **Positions:** Striker 50-55, fullbacks 40-45, box-to-box 40-45, pivot 30-35
- **Advantages:** Sustainable, organized shape, good counter-attack position, flexible
- **Disadvantages:** Not aggressive, not defensive, middle-ground risks
- **Recovery:** Reset to deep block if opposition breaks through

**System 3: DEEP BLOCK (Defensive, Own Half)**
- **Depth:** 70+ yards from opposition goal (own half)
- **When:** when chasing late, vs. possession teams, with injuries, defensive needs
- **Duration:** 45-90 minutes (entire match capable)
- **Positions:** All players drop deep, minimal pressing, compact shape
- **Advantages:** Maximum protection, sustainable, prevents penetration
- **Disadvantages:** Zero counter-attack opportunity, opposition control, boring
- **Application:** Chasing late match, injury situations

**System 4: PRESSING TRAPS (Organized Multi-Player Sequences)**
- **Trap 1:** Center-back trap (force fullback pass)
- **Trap 2:** Midfielder trap (force backward pass)
- **Trap 3:** Fullback trap (force inside pass)
- **Trap 4:** Wing trap (force central pass)
- **Design principle:** Multiple pressers from different angles, opposition limited options
- **Execution:** Organized (not random), coordinated timing

**System 5: GEGENPRESSING (Immediate Counter-Press After Losing Ball)**
- **Principle:** Lose ball → immediately press (don't reset)
- **Duration:** 20-30 minutes per match (exhausting, not entire match)
- **Execution:** Ball awareness, immediate pressing, organized trap, recovery discipline
- **Advantages:** Ball recovery near opposition goal, opponent discomfort, psychological advantage
- **Disadvantages:** Very high energy, high risk if press fails, timing-dependent
- **Recovery system:** Failed press → organized reset (mid-block)

**Energy Management Framework (Decision Based on Opponent + Situation):**

| Opposition | System | Duration | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Possession-dominant | Mid-block | 90 min | Efficiency over aggression |
| Direct-play | High press | 30 min then reset | Force short passing |
| Leading late | Mid-block | 15-20 min | Secure without risk |
| Chasing late | High press | 20 min | All-or-nothing approach |
| Injuries/fatigue | Deep block | 45+ min | Energy conservation |

**Pressing Trigger Assignment (Position-Specific):**
- Striker: Press center-back ONLY (not other positions)
- Fullbacks: Press opposition fullbacks (not center-backs)
- Box-to-box: Press opposition midfielders (midfield responsibility)
- CDM: Coverage (never primary press, selective only)
- Winger: Track opposition fullback (prevent overlap)

**Teaching Progression (4 Levels):**
1. System understanding (unopposed, clarity on each system)
2. System execution (semi-opposed, practice each system)
3. Traps & recovery (active opposition, trap coordination)
4. Match-realistic 11v11 (full integration, tactical adaptation)

**Application:**
- Tactical planning (system selection vs. opposition)
- Opposition analysis (determine opponent vulnerabilities)
- Energy management (pressing windows vs. sustainable pace)
- Pressing efficiency (success rate measurement)
