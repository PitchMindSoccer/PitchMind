## Research Methodology & Coverage

### 10 Dimensions Per Archetype

Each archetype profile covers:

1. **What makes them recognizable on video** (5-7 observable patterns)
   - Receiving posture / movement signatures
   - Finishing patterns / Distribution patterns
   - Space occupation
   - Technical distinctive markers

2. **Physical Characteristics**
   - Build type, height, weight, explosiveness, strength
   - Movement quality traits
   - Why the physical profile fits the archetype

3. **Cognitive & Technical Characteristics**
   - Decision-making hierarchy (priority order)
   - Technical execution standards
   - Key competencies

4. **Movement Patterns** (3-5 unique patterns)
   - Named patterns (e.g., "Drag-and-Drop," "Press-and-Recycle")
   - How each works, why it's powerful

5. **Decision-Making Under Pressure vs. In Space**
   - Observable sequences
   - Key differences

6. **Key Developmental Stages** (U-12 to U-18+)
   - What emerges at each stage
   - What coaches look for
   - Coaching focus

7. **How Elite Coaches Identify & Develop**
   - Identification criteria (youth level)
   - Development pathway
   - What elite coaches teach explicitly (with examples)

8. **Characteristic Weaknesses** (4-6 specific weaknesses)
   - Why inherent to the archetype
   - Real impact examples
   - When exposed

9. **Archetype Interactions** (5-8 pairings)
   - Why certain pairings work/don't work
   - Elite examples
   - Observable benefits

10. **Summary Table** (one-page reference guide)

## Key Research Insights

### Insight #1: The Blend Philosophy
**"These are not boxes — they are gravitational centers. Every real player is a blend."**

The best players often pull toward one archetype but have elements of another:
- Firmino: 72% Link-up Striker + 28% Complete Striker
- De Bruyne: 65% Progressive Carrier + 35% Creative Playmaker
- Kanté: 70% Pressing Mid + 30% Ball-Playing DM

Future PitchMind should focus on identifying and developing these blends.

### Insight #2: Observable Markers Are Consistent
What makes an archetype recognizable is **repeatable across games and contexts:**
- Link-up Strikers receive and pass in one touch 8+ times per game
- Channel Runners time runs to offside trap millisecond precision
- Registas show distinctive pre-scanning pattern before every receipt
- Progressive Carriers advance the team 20-30 yards in single carry 3-5 times per game

These patterns are trainable and coachable.

### Insight #3: Weaknesses Are Inherent
Each archetype's weakness is not a flaw but a consequence of their strength:
- Link-up Strikers score fewer because they pass first (strength = facilitator)
- Channel Runners are vulnerable to tight marking because they depend on space (strength = exploiter)
- Registas vulnerable to high press because they're deep (strength = orchestrator from back)

The role is the weakness; the weakness is the role.

### Insight #4: Development Pathways Are Clear
Elite coaches don't just identify archetypes — they develop them systematically:
- U-12-15: Foundation phase (technique, first understanding of role)
- U-15-18: Identity confirmation (physical emergence, pattern sophistication)
- U-18+: Refinement (consistency at high intensity, system adaptability)

At each stage, different skills are prioritized.

### Insight #5: System Integration Matters
No archetype exists in isolation. They interact:
- **Perfect pairings:** Link-up Striker + Poacher (one creates, one finishes)
- **Natural partnerships:** Regista + Box-to-Box Mid (distribution + athleticism balance)
- **Forced combinations:** Two poachers (conflict — both want to finish)

Understanding archetype interactions is crucial for team building.
