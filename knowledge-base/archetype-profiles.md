---

> **Philosophy:** These archetypes are not boxes — they are gravitational centers. Every real player is a blend. The system identifies which center pulls hardest, then shows where the player's orbit diverges. A good archetype profile doesn't label a player; it starts a conversation between them and their coach.

---

## HOW TO READ THESE PROFILES

**Weight Scale:**
- CRITICAL (3.0) — This IS the archetype. Scoring low here = you're not this type.
- HIGH (2.0) — Expected and important. Defines quality within the type.
- MEDIUM (1.0) — Baseline expectation. Average is fine here.
- LOW (0.5) — Present but not defining. Weakness here won't disqualify.
- NEGLIGIBLE (0.1) — Barely relevant. Score what you score.

**Expected Range:** The score band a "pure" version of this archetype typically lands in for each category (0–100 scale). Divergence = player score > 15 pts above the high end.

## ST — Striker Archetypes (4 types)

### ARCHETYPE: Link-up Striker
**Description:** The anti-poacher. Back-to-goal craftsman who connects midfield to attack through hold-up, layoffs, and combination play. Lives between the lines, receives under pressure, and makes everyone around him better. Goals are a byproduct, not the primary function.
**Elite examples:** Roberto Firmino (Liverpool), Karim Benzema (Real Madrid pre-2021), Francesco Totti (Roma), Llorente (Athletic Bilbao)
**Defining question:** "Do they make the team better even when not scoring?"

**Sub-category Weights (for archetype matching):**
| Sub-category | Weight | Expected Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shielding | CRITICAL (3.0) | 72–90 | The absolute core of this archetype |
| First Touch | CRITICAL (3.0) | 70–88 | Must control under pressure and set next action |
| Distribution | HIGH (2.0) | 65–84 | Layoffs, 1-2s, switching play to runners |
| Awareness | HIGH (2.0) | 65–82 | Scanning for runners, spotting press triggers |
| Positioning | HIGH (2.0) | 68–85 | Intelligent space occupation between lines |
| Decision Making | HIGH (2.0) | 68–84 | When to hold, when to release, when to turn |
| Reading the Game | MEDIUM (1.0) | 62–80 | Anticipates pressing triggers, finds pockets |
| Problem-Solving | MEDIUM (1.0) | 60–78 | Creative under pressure, escapes tight spaces |
| Concentration/Focus | MEDIUM (1.0) | 60–78 | Maintains composure when isolated |
| Dribbling | MEDIUM (1.0) | 48–68 | Useful in tight areas, not a dribbler by trade |
| Shooting | LOW (0.5) | 45–70 | Finishes when opportunities arise, not a poacher |
| Tackling | LOW (0.5) | 30–55 | Not expected to press or tackle aggressively |

**Top 3 defining sub-sub categories:**
1. **Hold-up play** (Shielding) — Back to goal, pins CB, absorbs contact, buys time for runners to arrive
2. **Layoff timing** (Distribution) — The wall-pass instinct; instant release into space behind or beside
3. **Space creation off-ball** (Positioning) — Drags CB out of position, pins defensive line, opens channels for third-man runners

**Divergence triggers (suggests secondary archetype blending):**
- Shooting > 78 AND Dribbling > 72 → **Channel Runner traits** detected
- Dribbling > 80 → **False 9 traits** detected
- Positioning > 85 AND Reading > 82 → **Complete Striker traits** bleeding in
- Distribution > 85 AND Awareness > 82 → **False 9 / Playmaking** traits detected

**Growth paths:**
- **Path A — Sharpen Link-up:** Wall passes in tight spaces (2-touch max), back-to-goal hold-up vs. resistance, layoff timing in 3-man combination drills, scanning routine before receiving
- **Path B — If Channel Runner blend:** Diagonal run timing in behind last line, first touch in stride, finishing in stride from secondary positions

### ARCHETYPE: Channel Runner / Poacher

| Problem-Solving | HIGH (2.0) | 70–88 | Creates from chaos, invents solutions in real time |
| Reading the Game | HIGH (2.0) | 68–86 | Reads when to vacate #9 position, trigger moments |
| First Touch | HIGH (2.0) | 70–88 | Must control in midfield zones under pressure |
| Positioning | MEDIUM (1.0) | 55–78 | Fluid — not channel running, but disrupting shape |

**Top 3 defining sub-sub categories:**
1. **Deep receipt and carry** (Dribbling + First Touch) — Receives 30m from goal and drives at defenders — the Messi movement
2. **Third-man activation** (Distribution + Awareness) — The pass that unlocks runners who arrive from deep

**Divergence triggers:**

### ARCHETYPE: Creative Playmaker (The Classic 10)
**Description:** The metronome of the attack. Sees the pass before it exists, operates in half-spaces, and controls tempo through touch, vision, and weight of pass. Not necessarily the press leader — more a chess player who moves pieces. The game flows through them.
**Elite examples:** Kevin De Bruyne (Man City), Mesut Özil (Arsenal/Real Madrid), Andrés Iniesta (Barcelona), Juan Román Riquelme
**Defining question:** "Do attacks become better when they touch the ball?"

**Sub-category Weights (for archetype matching):**
| Sub-category | Weight | Expected Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Distribution | CRITICAL (3.0) | 78–95 | This is everything — weight, angle, timing of passes |
| Awareness | CRITICAL (3.0) | 75–92 | 360° scanning, sees all options before the ball arrives |
| Decision Making | HIGH (2.0) | 74–92 | Chooses the right pass at the right moment |
| Reading the Game | HIGH (2.0) | 72–90 | Anticipates defensive shape and triggers |
| First Touch | HIGH (2.0) | 72–90 | Sets the next action; poor touch = lost tempo |
| Problem-Solving | HIGH (2.0) | 68–88 | Invents solutions in dense, pressing environments |
| Positioning | MEDIUM (1.0) | 62–82 | Finds half-spaces, receives between lines |
| Dribbling | MEDIUM (1.0) | 58–78 | Dribbles to create passing angles, not to beat men |
| Concentration/Focus | MEDIUM (1.0) | 65–82 | Maintains focus through low-touch periods |
| Shooting | LOW (0.5) | 45–72 | Can score but it's not the primary job |
| Shielding | LOW (0.5) | 45–68 | Protects when pressed, not a hold-up player |
| Tackling | NEGLIGIBLE (0.1) | 25–48 | Minimal defensive contribution expected |

**Top 3 defining sub-sub categories:**
1. **Weight and angle of through ball** (Distribution) — The De Bruyne laser: perfect weight, perfect timing, unlocks any defense
2. **Pre-scan before receipt** (Awareness) — Iniesta's head always moving; knows where everyone is before the ball arrives
3. **Half-space positioning** (Positioning + Awareness) — Gravitates to the channel between fullback and CB, where space lives

**Divergence triggers:**
- Shooting > 76 AND Dribbling > 78 → **Goal-Threat 10 / Trequartista** traits
- Dribbling > 82 → **Direct 10** or **False 9** blend
- Tackling > 55 AND Positioning > 80 → **Press-and-Combine** traits bleeding in

**Growth paths:**
- **Path A — Refine Playmaker:** Through-ball weight drills under pressure (2-touch max), switching-play training from half-space receives, scanning routine audit with coach video review
- **Path B — If Goal-Threat blend:** Shooting from edge of box after dribble, cutting inside from halfspace, combination finishing (overlap + cut-back)

### ARCHETYPE: Inverted Winger
**Description:** Comes from the "wrong" side — right footed on the left, left footed on the right. Cuts inside to shoot, combine, or play through balls. Their wide starting position is a decoy; their real threat is always diagonal and central. The modern winger template.
**Elite examples:** Mohamed Salah (Liverpool, right-footed on left), Arjen Robben (right-footed on left), Thierry Henry, Neymar
**Defining question:** "Does their wide starting position create central danger?"

|---|---|---|---|
| Dribbling | CRITICAL (3.0) | 76–94 | Cut inside movement, tight control at speed |
| Shooting | CRITICAL (3.0) | 72–92 | The inside-cut ends in a shot — must finish |
| Decision Making | HIGH (2.0) | 72–90 | Cut in or cross? Always a read required |
| First Touch | HIGH (2.0) | 70–88 | Controls wide, sets the cut — first touch IS the start |
| Positioning | HIGH (2.0) | 68–86 | Wide starting position, half-space arrival timing |
| Awareness | MEDIUM (1.0) | 62–82 | Sees the overlap, knows when keeper is off his line |
| Reading the Game | MEDIUM (1.0) | 60–80 | Reads defensive shape to decide timing of cut |
| Problem-Solving | MEDIUM (1.0) | 58–78 | Creates from nothing when 1st option is denied |
| Distribution | LOW (0.5) | 45–70 | Crosses occasionally but it's not the primary tool |
| Shielding | LOW (0.5) | 42–65 | Protects when pressed to set up cut |
| Concentration/Focus | MEDIUM (1.0) | 62–80 | Stays dangerous through quiet spells |

**Top 3 defining sub-sub categories:**
1. **Inside cut to shoot** (Dribbling + Shooting) — The signature move: receive wide, one touch inside, low drive across keeper
2. **Half-space arrival** (Positioning + Decision Making) — The run from the wide zone into the half-space that creates shooting angles
3. **Lateral dribble at pace** (Dribbling + First Touch) — Takes the defender on the outside then cuts — or fakes outside and cuts immediately

**Divergence triggers:**

**Growth paths:**

### Step 3: Calculate Weighted Distance for Each Archetype
```python
def calculate_archetype_match(player_scores, archetype):
    weighted_distance = 0
    for category, config in archetype.items():
        weight = config["weight"]
        ideal = config["ideal"]
        player_score = player_scores[category]
        distance = abs(player_score - ideal)
        weighted_distance += distance * weight
    
    # Convert distance to match score (closer = higher score)
    match_score = 1 / (1 + weighted_distance / 100)
    return match_score
```

### Step 4: Normalize Across All Archetypes for the Position
```python
def normalize_match_scores(position_archetypes, player_scores):
    raw_scores = {}
    for archetype_name, archetype_config in position_archetypes.items():
        raw_scores[archetype_name] = calculate_archetype_match(player_scores, archetype_config)
    
    total = sum(raw_scores.values())
    normalized = {k: (v / total) * 100 for k, v in raw_scores.items()}
    
    # Sort descending
    sorted_archetypes = sorted(normalized.items(), key=lambda x: x[1], reverse=True)
    return sorted_archetypes
```
