# SESSION 018: Team Shape Effects on Individual Player Decisions
## How Formation Changes What is "Correct" for Individual Players

**Session Date:** March 3, 2026  
**Research Focus:** How formation shapes (4-3-3 vs 4-4-2 vs 4-2-3-1 vs 3-5-2) change pressing triggers, positioning decisions, and defensive responsibilities for individual players.

---

## Executive Summary

Formation shape is not a cosmetic difference—it fundamentally rewires individual player decision-making at every phase of play.

The same midfielder in a **4-3-3** vs **4-4-2** has:
- Different pressing trigger points
- Different cover responsibilities
- Different passing lanes to exploit
- Different pressing angles available
- Different recovery run priorities

**Key insight:** A decision that is "correct" in one formation may be a tactical error in another. Scoring must account for formation context.

---

## Part 1: Formation Shape Fundamentals

### Why Formation Shape Matters

Formation defines:
1. **Vertical compactness** (depth relationships between lines)
2. **Horizontal compactness** (lateral spacing within lines)
3. **Center of gravity** (how far forward the team sits)
4. **Defensive trigger points** (when/where pressing starts)
5. **Passing lane availability** (what options are "open")
6. **Cover shadow geometry** (who blocks what passing lane)
7. **Numerical relationships** (advantage/disadvantage in each zone)

### The Three Critical Zones

Every formation creates three zones with different densities:

| Zone | Definition | Formation Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Build-up zone** (defensive third) | Where team starts with possession | 4-3-3: 2 center-backs + 1 CDM vs opposition striker; numerical advantage |
| **Midfield transition** | Where press is triggered & sustained | Formation determines who presses first; changes pressing angle/trigger |
| **Final third** | Where attacks are completed | Formation determines width sources & overload possibilities |

## Part 2: Deep Dive — 4-3-3 Formation

### What is a 4-3-3?

**Structure:** 4 defenders | 1 defensive midfielder (CDM) + 2 center midfielders (CMs) | 2 wingers + 1 striker

**Key feature:** Single pivot CDM sits directly in front of defense. Two CMs operate in half-spaces or inside channels.

### In-Possession Responsibilities (4-3-3)

#### Defenders' Decisions
- **Center-backs:** Primary build-up role. Access central midfield overload (3v1 vs striker initially).
- **Full-backs:** Push forward to provide width once ball enters midfield.
- **Key decision:** When to push forward vs when to hold back as safety valve.

#### CDM's Role (Single Pivot)
- **Primary responsibility:** Stay deep, protect space between back line and advanced midfielders.
- **Passing responsibility:** Distribute to CMs, wingers, or full-backs. Orchestrate switches of play.
- **Coverage:** Must cover both half-spaces if CMs push forward.
- **Key decision:** When to push forward into midfield vs stay as security blanket.

#### Center Midfielders' Decisions
- **Positioning:** Occupy half-spaces/inside channels, not wing-back lines.
- **Passing lanes:** Look for winger runs (overlap), striker drop-off, diagonal carries.
- **Key decision:** Press into final third with winger or hold a passing option deeper?
- **Movement:** Can make forward runs if one stays back to cover CDM.

#### Wingers' Decisions
- **Positioning:** Start narrow (to block center), can move wide to stretch defense.
- **Key decision:** Hold wide position to create space for overlapping FB, OR tuck inside to create space for advanced CM?
- **Role duality:** Both attacking threats AND defensive pressers.

#### Striker's Role
- **Movement:** Pin defender, drop short to create overload, drift wide to create space.
- **Key decision:** Receive ball deep or make runs in behind?

### Out-of-Possession (Pressing Structure)

#### High Press in 4-3-3

**Front three form:** 4-1-4-1 block (compact, narrow)
- Striker presses ball carrier
- Wingers start narrow to block central passes
- Pressing trigger: Immediate (ball is in build-up)

**Midfield three adapt:**
- CDM stays deep, covers between pressing lines
- Two CMs either:
  - **Narrow option:** Form 4-1-4-1, block central areas aggressively
  - **Wide option:** Move to half-spaces to press from wider angles

**Individual decision points:**
1. **When striker presses:** Do the wingers stay narrow to cut passing lanes, or move wide to press the fullback?
2. **Half-space occupation:** Which CM presses the ball carrier? The other provides cover.
3. **Cover shadow geometry:** CMs position diagonally to block passes between the lines (into attacking space behind their line).

#### Medium/Low Block in 4-3-3

**Structure:** 4-3-3 shape maintained (slightly narrower)
- CDM sits directly in front of back four
- Two CMs occupy half-spaces, narrower than possession phase
- Wingers track back, help with wide defense

**Pressing decision triggers:**
- **When to press a fullback:** Full-back has time? Only press if winger can support.
- **When to press a CM:** If ball carrier is turning sideways, press immediately. If they're facing forward, delay.
- **Recovery run priority:** If press is beaten, fastest player (usually a CM) sprints back to cover space.

## Part 3: 4-4-2 Formation

### What is a 4-4-2?

**Structure:** 4 defenders | 2 center midfielders (no true CDM) | 2 wide midfielders/wingers | 2 strikers

**Key feature:** No defensive midfielder. Both CMs share defensive load; formation is more "box-to-box" focused.

### In-Possession Differences vs 4-3-3

#### Midfield Structure
- **No single pivot:** Both CMs are required to balance defense & attack.
- **Width sources:** Wide midfielders provide width naturally (not dependent on full-back overlaps).
- **Build-up imbalance:** Often requires full-back to step into midfield to create 3v1 advantage in build-up.
- **Key difference:** More structured, less flexible positional play.

#### Individual Decision Changes

| Player | 4-3-3 Decision | 4-4-2 Decision |
|---|---|---|
| **LCM (Left CM)** | Can move into attacking third if RCM covers; operates in half-space | Must maintain defensive position; can't both push forward; more conservative |
| **RCM (Right CM)** | Similar flexibility | Same restriction |
| **Wingers** | Narrow start, create space for overlapping FB | Wide start, provide immediate width; less need for FB overlap |
| **Full-backs** | Required to provide width | Can operate more defensively; less required to push forward |
| **Strikers (two)** | Single ST has more positional freedom | Two STs must coordinate; one often checks to ball, other stays in behind |

### Out-of-Possession (4-4-2 Pressing)

#### High Press in 4-4-2

**Front two press:**
- Both strikers aggressively press center-backs
- Leaves space behind if press is broken
- **Decision point:** Do wingers track fullbacks aggressively or sit in a 4-4-2 block?

**Midfield pressing:**
- Two CMs cannot both go forward (no cover available)
- One CM must stay back to shield defense
- **Decision point:** Which CM steps up to press? Usually the one closer to ball.

#### Key 4-4-2 Vulnerability

If one striker presses CB and other drops to support press-resistant build-up, the midfield can be outnumbered quickly.

**Solution:** Wingers must track fullbacks immediately (4-4-2 is vulnerable on flanks in high press).

## Part 4: 4-2-3-1 Formation

### What is a 4-2-3-1?

**Structure:** 4 defenders | 2 defensive midfielders | 3 advanced midfielders (attacking mids, shadow striker, winger roles) | 1 striker

**Key feature:** Double pivot of two DMs in front of defense. More defensive security than 4-3-3.

### Key Differences from 4-3-3

#### In-Possession

| Aspect | 4-3-3 | 4-2-3-1 |
|---|---|---|
| **Midfield depth** | 1 CDM + 2 CMs (unbalanced depth) | 2 DMs (equal depth, flat) |
| **Build-up advantage** | 3v1 (CBs + CDM + one CM vs ST) | 2v1 (CBs + one DM vs ST) |
| **Attacking player positioning** | CMs in half-spaces, wingers on flanks | 3 attacking mids between midfield & strikers |
| **Flexibility** | High (CMs can push forward independently) | Lower (DMs must move together) |
| **Passing options** | Varied: wingers, CMs, strikers | More constrained: must go through DMs or wingers |

#### Pressing Decision Changes

**High press in 4-2-3-1:**
- Striker presses CB (same as 4-3-3)
- But pressing team has 3 advanced mids (not 2 wingers)
- **Key difference:** More midfielders can press forward simultaneously
- **Risk:** If all 3 advanced mids go forward, 2 DMs must cover entire midfield (stretched thin)

**Decision trigger for 4-2-3-1 pressing:**
- Are one of the 3 advanced mids out of position (e.g., tucked too narrow)?
- If yes, can that player still track back to defensive duties?
- Unlike 4-3-3, you can't leave both advanced mids out of position simultaneously

## Part 5: 3-5-2 Formation

### What is a 3-5-2?

**Structure:** 3 center-backs | 2 wing-backs (fullback + midfielder hybrid) + 1 center midfielder | 2 attacking midfielders/wingers | 2 strikers

**Key feature:** Wing-backs are the key—they must defend & attack wide areas.

### In-Possession Role Changes

#### Wing-backs (vs Full-backs in 4-3-3)

| Aspect | Full-back (4-3-3) | Wing-back (3-5-2) |
|---|---|---|
| **Build-up role** | Support center-back; provide width on overlap | Key playmaker; must drop into midfield to create 4v1 build-up advantage |
| **Forward role** | Push forward to support winger | Much wider runs; create width at more advanced positions |
| **Defensive role** | Mark opposing fullback/winger | Cover entire flank (wider responsibility) |
| **Decision load** | Moderate (clear role boundaries) | Very high (must switch between roles constantly) |

#### Center Midfielders in 3-5-2

- **Fewer of them:** Usually 1 CM (sometimes 2 in build-up)
- **Positioning:** Must move vertically to cover wing-back when they attack
- **Decision:** When wing-back attacks wide, CM must choose: cover space left behind, or press forward?

### Pressing Structure (3-5-2)

#### High Press in 3-5-2

**Two strikers press:**
- Aggressively press CBs/fullbacks
- Angle press to force play to one side

**Wing-backs' decision:**
- If opposing fullback has the ball: Do I press high or stay back to cover winger runs?
- Unlike 4-3-3 wingers (who press automatically), 3-5-2 wing-backs must choose

**Center back decision:**
- If wing-back presses high, which CB shifts to cover that flank?
- Decision point: Wider CB steps out, or middle CB shifts across?

## Part 6: Critical Decision-Making Framework

### Pressing Trigger Recognition

The **same situation** requires different responses based on formation:

#### Scenario: Opposition fullback has the ball on the flank

**4-3-3 response:**
- Winger presses automatically (closes down)
- Full-back provides covering support
- CDM watches space centrally
- **Outcome:** Quick, compact pressure

**4-4-2 response:**
- Wide midfielder presses (not winger from a position of width)
- Other CM must stay back (no CDM to cover)
- **Outcome:** More cautious; less numerical advantage in press

**3-5-2 response:**
- Wing-back decision: Do I press the fullback or cover space?
- If wing-back presses, a CB must cover that flank
- **Outcome:** Highest risk/reward (can win ball quickly or leave flank exposed)

### Passing Lane Recognition

Formation changes which passes are "dangerous" and which are "safe."

#### Example: Opposition strikes a diagonal pass

**4-3-3:** 
- If diagonal targets the half-space behind the CM line, it's dangerous (bypass midfield)
- CDM responsibility: Read this anticipatorily, position to cut out the pass

**4-4-2:**
- Diagonal targeting half-space is equally dangerous but less likely
- Formation is more compact, fewer passing options available

**3-5-2:**
- Diagonal into half-space is dangerous, but wing-back may have already moved forward
- Covering CB must read the pass early

### Cover Shadow Positioning

**Cover shadow:** The imaginary space "behind" a pressing player that blocks passing lanes.

#### 4-3-3 Example: CDM Pressing

- CDM presses the opposition's central midfielder
- Cover shadow blocks passes into the advanced midfielder (dangerous zone)
- This is a diagonal cover shadow (from lower-left to upper-right)

**Scoring implication:** If CDM is out of position, the cover shadow disappears, and the opposition can freely pass into advanced positions. This is a decision error specific to the 4-3-3 structure.

#### 4-4-2 Example: CM Pressing

- One CM presses the ball carrier
- Other CM must provide vertical cover (not the same geometry as 4-3-3)
- Cover shadow is more vertical (straight back)

**Scoring implication:** Both CMs pressing = catastrophic; no cover shadow at all. So pressing triggers must be more conservative in 4-4-2.

## Part 7: Formation-Specific Role Archetypes

### CDM in 4-3-3

**Decision-making priorities (in order):**
1. **Safety first:** Are advanced midfielders too far forward? Stay back.
2. **Tempo control:** Can I step forward to receive & dictate play? Move up.
3. **Press recovery:** If winger loses ball high up, sprint back.
4. **Pressing jump:** Can I press forward if the situation permits? Less common than CMs.

**Scoring adjustment:**
- CDM who presses too aggressively in a 4-3-3 = decision error (no cover available)
- CDM who steps forward to collect ball when CMs are also advanced = risky but potentially brilliant

### CMs in 4-3-3

**Decision-making priorities:**
1. **Half-space occupation:** Stay in attacking half-space to receive diagonal passes.
2. **Forward runs:** Can I drive forward into final third? Yes, if other CM stays back.
3. **Pressing support:** Press if the winger is committed; otherwise stay back.
4. **Box coverage:** Make near-post runs to support striker.

**Scoring adjustment:**
- CM who presses while other CM is also advanced AND CDM is not in cover position = major decision error
- CM who makes a forward run while covering for CDM = positive decision (creates overload in attacking third)

### Wingers in 4-3-3

**Decision-making priorities:**
1. **Width vs infield:** Start narrow (block center), move wide based on fullback positioning.
2. **Pressing trigger:** Do I press the fullback or sit narrow to cut central passes?
3. **Box coverage:** Near-post runs if striker is playing on the shoulder; far-post if striker dropped deep.
4. **Defensive tracking:** Priority = track fullback when possession is lost.

**Scoring adjustment:**
- Winger who sits too narrow (blocks own full-back's passing options) = decision error
- Winger who tracks fullback effectively = positive decision

### Wing-backs in 3-5-2

**Decision-making priorities (highest load):**
1. **Deep or high?** Is the opposite fullback threatening my flank? Stay back or push forward?
2. **Who covers me?** Which CB will cover if I push forward? Coordinate movement.
3. **Build-up vs defense:** Do I drop to build-up (create 4v1) or stay wide to support press?
4. **Second-phase:** After losing ball, recovery run priority = diagonal back to cover flank.

**Scoring adjustment:**
- Wing-back who pushes too high without CB cover = decision error (leaves flank exposed)
- Wing-back who times their push with center-midfielder cover = positive (creates width advantage)

## Part 8: Decision-Making Under Different Pressing Scenarios

### Scenario 1: High Press on Opposition Build-Up

**4-3-3 approach:**
1. Striker presses CB
2. Wingers start narrow, ready to press fullback if CB plays wide
3. CDM watches space centrally (doesn't press initially)
4. CMs can press the passing targets (if they move forward)

**4-4-2 approach:**
1. Both strikers press CMs (not CBs directly—no numerical advantage in build-up)
2. Wingers track fullbacks tight (primary defensive duty)
3. Both CMs stay deeper than 4-3-3 (no CDM available for cover)

**3-5-2 approach:**
1. Strikers press CBs aggressively (angle press to one side)
2. Wing-backs decide: Stay back to cover wingers OR push to press the passing targets
3. Central midfielder must cover for wing-back if they press

**Scoring implications:**
- In 4-3-3, a winger who fails to press the fullback = decision error
- In 4-4-2, a winger who presses the fullback too aggressively (leaving space in midfield) = decision error
- In 3-5-2, a wing-back who presses without CB cover = decision error

## Part 9: Positional Adjustments Across Formations

### Half-Space Utilization

**Half-space:** The space between the center and the flank (inside channel).

| Formation | Half-space Usage |
|---|---|
| **4-3-3** | Two CMs occupy half-spaces; they're the secondary playmaking option |
| **4-4-2** | CMs are closer to full-backs; half-space is less emphasized |
| **4-2-3-1** | Three advanced mids use half-spaces heavily; more complex movement |
| **3-5-2** | Wing-backs create width; half-space is less central to play |

**Scoring adjustment:**
- A CM in a 4-3-3 who plays too wide (abandons half-space) = decision error
- A 4-2-3-1 advanced mid who occupies half-space but doesn't receive = wasted positioning (minor decision error)
- A wing-back in 3-5-2 who sits in half-space = decision error (should be on flank or deep)

## Part 10: Formation-Specific Fatigue Curves

### How Formation Affects Position-Specific Fatigue

**4-3-3 fatigue profile:**
- CDM: Heavily loaded (constant covering), visible fatigue from minute 65+
- CMs: Moderate load (can rotate effort), fatigue visible from minute 70+
- Wingers: High load (must both attack & defend), fatigue visible from minute 60+

**4-4-2 fatigue profile:**
- CMs: Very high load (no CDM to share), visible fatigue from minute 55+
- Wingers: Highest load (must defend entire flank), visible fatigue from minute 50+

**4-2-3-1 fatigue profile:**
- DMs: Moderately high load (two players share), fatigue visible from minute 70+
- Advanced mids: Highest load (must cover more ground), fatigue visible from minute 65+

**3-5-2 fatigue profile:**
- Wing-backs: Extremely high load (highest of any position in any formation), visible fatigue from minute 45+
- Center midfielder: High load, fatigue visible from minute 65+

**Scoring implication:** The same player in the same position fatigues at different rates depending on formation. A 4-4-2 winger should be scored 5-10% lower from minute 55+ than a 4-3-3 winger.

## Part 11: Building Out Against Different Pressures

### 4-3-3 vs 4-2-3-1 in Build-Up

**4-3-3 has numerical advantage in build-up (3v1: two CBs + CDM vs opposition ST)**

Individual decision changes:
- CDM can drop into center-back line (becomes 4v1)
- Full-back can move into midfield to create 5v2
- One CM can move very high, creating overload opportunities

**4-2-3-1 has less advantage in build-up (2v1: two CBs vs opposition ST)**

Individual decision changes:
- Must be more careful with the ball (fewer safety valves)
- Wing-back must help build-up (creates 3v1)
- CMs cannot both push forward (less flexibility)

**Scoring adjustment:**
- A 4-3-3 CD who plays a dangerous long pass = decision error (numerical advantage available)
- A 4-2-3-1 CB who plays a long pass in similar situation = acceptable risk (fewer options available)

### 4-3-3 vs 3-5-2 Attacking a High Press

**4-3-3 solution:**
- Beat the cover shadow with an advanced midfielder (Girona FC approach)
- Full-back moves closer to center-backs, advanced CM makes a long run outside winger to receive

**3-5-2 solution:**
- Wing-back drops into build-up (creates width), allows switching play
- OR wing-back pushes forward to attack the overloaded pressing area

**Scoring adjustment:**
- A 4-3-3 CM who makes a long run outside the winger to beat cover shadow = positive decision
- A 3-5-2 wing-back who moves without communicating who covers them = decision error

## Part 12: Elite Coach Philosophies on Formation & Decision-Making

### Guardiola's 4-3-3 (Barcelona, Bayern, City)

**Philosophy:** Positional superiority through midfield overloads.

**Individual decision changes:**
- Advanced midfielders have high license to move (create overloads)
- CDM stays very deep (covers for aggressive CMs)
- Wingers can invert into attacking midfield zones
- Pressing is highly coordinated (cover shadows are geometry-based, not player-based)

**Implication:** Guardiola's players press based on where to block passing lanes, not marking individuals.

### Klopp's 4-3-3 (Liverpool)

**Philosophy:** Quick transitions, high pressing intensity, fullback width.

**Individual decision changes:**
- Wingers cut inside (create space for full-back overlaps)
- CMs provide immediate pressure in midfield (not waiting for triggers)
- Pressing is intense, not always positionally perfect (chaos management)
- Full-backs must recover quickly if press is beaten

**Implication:** Klopp's players press first, ask questions later.

### Rangnick's 4-2-3-1 (RB Leipzig, Manchester United)

**Philosophy:** Structured pressing, numerical advantages, transitions.

**Individual decision changes:**
- Both DMs press as a unit (never separated)
- Advanced mids have strict pressing triggers (not always immediate)
- Transitions emphasize outlet passes to full-backs
- Formation rarely changes shape (very rigid)

**Implication:** Rangnick's players press based on training cues, not adaptive awareness.

## Part 13: Conversion Framework for Scoring

### How to Adjust Individual Scores Based on Formation

#### Step 1: Identify the Formation
- Is this clearly a 4-3-3 (three-person midfield, single pivot visible)?
- Or 4-4-2 (two CMs, two wingers, no defensive mid)?
- Or 4-2-3-1 (flat double pivot)?
- Or 3-5-2 (wing-backs, three CBs)?

#### Step 2: Identify the Player's Role in That Formation
- Is the winger playing wide or narrow?
- Is the CM in a half-space or a wide area?
- Is the fullback/wing-back pushing forward or staying back?

#### Step 3: Determine Formation-Specific Pressure
**Apply context-specific expectations:**

| Formation | Position | Pressing Intensity | Coverage Responsibility | Fatigue Adjustment (75+min) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4-3-3 | Winger | High | Full-back covers | Standard |
| 4-3-3 | CDM | Medium | Covers both CMs | Standard |
| 4-4-2 | CM | High | Minimal backup | -5% |
| 4-4-2 | Winger | Very High | Responsible for flank | -10% |
| 4-2-3-1 | DM | Medium | Covers with other DM | Standard |
| 4-2-3-1 | Advanced mid | Very High | Depends on press | -5% |
| 3-5-2 | Wing-back | Very High | No dedicated cover | -15% |
| 3-5-2 | CB | High | Covers wing-back flank | Standard |

#### Step 4: Decision Evaluation
**Example decision evaluation:**

**Situation:** Winger receives ball 30 yards from goal, three options:
1. Pass inside to CM (safe)
2. Attempt 1v1 dribble against fullback (high risk/reward)
3. Lay off to full-back (depends on formation)

| Formation | Correct Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **4-3-3** | Option 2 or 3 (depends on full-back position) | Full-back is prepared to overlap; 1v1 is expected role; passing to full-back is understood option |
| **4-4-2** | Option 1 (pass inside) | Limited full-back support; 1v1 is risky (no cover if turnover); must maintain structure |
| **3-5-2** | Option 2 (1v1 dribble) | Wing-back is trained for 1v1; full-back support is high likelihood; risk is acceptable |

**Scoring:** If winger passes inside in a 4-3-3, score 0.5 (safe but misses formation advantage). If winger attempts 1v1 in 4-4-2 and loses, score 0.2 (correct risk evaluation would be pass inside).

## Part 14: Youth Development Implications

### Formation Introduction Age Groups

| Age Group | Formations Taught | Decision Complexity |
|---|---|---|
| **U10-U12** | Mostly 4-4-2, some 3-3-4 | Minimal (simple roles) |
| **U12-U15** | 4-3-3, 4-4-2, basic 4-2-3-1 | Moderate (role-specific decisions) |
| **U15-U18** | All formations, asymmetrical variations | High (context-adaptive decisions) |

**Coaching implication:** A U12 player cannot be fairly scored on 4-3-3 CDM decisions if they've only trained 4-4-2. Formation familiarity is prerequisite for decision-making scoring.

## Part 15: Key Takeaways for PitchMind Scoring

### 1. Formation Matters As Much As Position

**Old thinking:** "Score a CM as a CM regardless of formation."

**Correct thinking:** "Score a CM differently depending on formation (4-3-3 CDM vs 4-4-2 CM are different roles with different decision-making frameworks)."

### 2. Pressing Trigger Varies by Formation

| Formation | Pressing Philosophy |
|---|---|
| **4-3-3** | Positional (block passing lanes) |
| **4-4-2** | Compact (stay tight, press as unit) |
| **4-2-3-1** | Structured (DMs move together) |
| **3-5-2** | Wing-back dependent (varies by situation) |

### 3. Fatigue Effects Are Formation-Specific

- 4-4-2 wingers fatigue faster than 4-3-3 wingers (more defensive load)
- 3-5-2 wing-backs fatigue fastest (extremely high load)
- Adjust fatigue multipliers accordingly (minute 70+ is critical)

### 4. Cover Shadow Geometry Changes

- 4-3-3 CDM creates diagonal cover shadows
- 4-4-2 CMs create vertical cover shadows
- 3-5-2 wing-backs create flank-specific cover shadows

### 5. Half-Space Utilization Changes

- 4-3-3 emphasizes half-spaces (two CMs key to play)
- 4-4-2 emphasizes wide areas (wingers responsible for width)
- 4-2-3-1 emphasizes movement between lines (advanced mids key)
- 3-5-2 emphasizes flank play (wing-backs dominate width)

## Part 19: Quick Reference — Formation Decision-Making Guide

### Decision Type 1: "Should I Press?"

| Formation | Pressing Rule | Exception |
|---|---|---|
| **4-3-3** | Press if winger is committed. CDM stays back. | CDM can press if both CMs stay deep |
| **4-4-2** | One CM presses, one stays back. Both never leave simultaneously. | High press: both CMs can press if wingers track fullbacks |
| **4-2-3-1** | DMs move together (never separated). Advanced mids press frequently. | One DM can press if other is absolutely locked-in on marking |
| **3-5-2** | Wing-back decision-dependent. If WB presses, CB must cover. | Strikers can always press; they have CB cover |

### Decision Type 2: "Where Should I Position?"

| Formation | Positioning Rule |
|---|---|
| **4-3-3** | CMs in half-spaces; wingers wide or narrow based on fullback; CDM straight back |
| **4-4-2** | CMs closer to fullbacks; wingers more naturally wide; no deep defender in midfield |
| **4-2-3-1** | DMs flat (side-by-side); advanced mids between lines; occupied space between midfield & strikers |
| **3-5-2** | Wing-backs on flanks or deep in build-up; CB central; 1 CM (if that); 2 strikers high |

### Decision Type 3: "What Pass Should I Make?"

| Formation | Passing Priority |
|---|---|
| **4-3-3** | Through half-space to CM > Wide to full-back > Back to CDM |
| **4-4-2** | Lateral to fellow CM > Wide to winger > Back (fewer options) |
| **4-2-3-1** | Through DM > Into advanced mid in half-space > Switch play |
| **3-5-2** | Through CB/DM > To wing-back > To striker |
