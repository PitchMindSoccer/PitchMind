# Coaching Concept: Off-Ball Movement & Positioning

## What it is
Off-ball movement refers to player positioning and movement when they don't have the ball. It's about:
- **Positioning to receive** in dangerous/advantageous areas
- **Creating space for teammates** by moving defenders away
- **Supporting teammates** who have the ball
- **Occupying defenders** even when not directly involved
- **Reading the play** and moving to next relevant position

Off-ball movement is often overlooked but separates elite teams from average teams. Great teams have 9-10 players constantly making intelligent movements; average teams have most players stationary.

## Why it matters (player development angle)
Young players focus on the ball because that's where the action seems to be. But elite soccer is 90% positioning and 10% technical execution. A player who positions themselves poorly but technically executes perfectly still makes poor decisions. A player who positions themselves perfectly can execute simple technical actions and create chaos for opposition.

Young players need to understand:
- **Why** they move (to help teammate with ball, to create space, to threaten goal)
- **When** to move (while teammate is receiving, while ball is in transition, before pass is played)
- **Where** to move (into space, away from defender, between lines, behind opposition line)
- **How much** to move (too much = takes you out of play; too little = irrelevant)

## What GOOD looks like

**Ball carrier support (while teammate has ball):**
- At least 2 players are within 5-10 meters of ball carrier as passing options
- Support players are positioned to receive in forward/sideways directions (not just behind)
- Support players are not static—they're moving slightly to create receiving angle
- If one passing option is closed, another is automatically opening

**Space creation (movement without the ball):**
- Fullback runs down the line, pulling opposition fullback out of the midfield (creates space in midfield)
- Winger makes diagonal run, drawing centerback attention, opens space for striker
- Midfielder drops deep, pulls pressing forward, creates space for attacks higher up
- Forward makes sideways run, occupies centerback's attention, creates space for partner

**Attacking runs (getting into dangerous areas):**
- Runs are timed to coincide with when ball will be available (not too early, not too late)
- Runs are into space or gaps between defenders, not into congestion
- Multiple players make runs simultaneously, creating defensive overload
- Runs are varied (sometimes deep, sometimes lateral, sometimes checking back) so defenders can't predict

**Positioning (structural/positional movement):**
- Players maintain correct distances between each other (5-15 meters depending on system)
- Defensive line adjusts based on opposition movement (steps up as team attacks higher, drops as team transitions)
- Fullbacks reposition based on possession zone (wider when team attacks down their flank, narrower in opposite flanks)
- Pivot adjusts depth based on opposition pressure and distance to goal

## What POOR looks like
- **Stationary movement**: Players stand still waiting for ball instead of moving to receive/support
- **Bunching**: Too many players in same zone, creates congestion and reduces passing options
- **Lack of depth**: All attacking players in same line; defenders can organize easily
- **Lack of width**: All players central; opposition can compress easily
- **Isolation**: Teammate with ball has no receiving options, forced to turn back or lose possession
- **Poor run timing**: Runs too early (defender can step up and mark) or too late (ball played before run is complete)
- **Running into pressure**: Runs into crowded areas instead of running into space
- **Disconnection**: Defensive line is 30+ meters from midfield; too much space for opposition to play through
- **No reaction to ball movement**: When ball moves, players don't reposition; creates static ineffective shape

## Scenario examples

**Scenario A: Fullback has ball on wing, striker is being marked tightly**
- GOOD: Midfielder makes run toward goal along wing, pulling opposing midfielder, creating space for striker's partner
- GOOD: Striker's partner makes diagonal run away from fullback's press, ready to receive in space
- POOR: Striker continues to run into centerback's marking, not moving; fullback has no pass options
- POOR: Midfielder stands still in midfield; fullback has no escape pass when winger press arrives

**Scenario B: Goalkeeper has ball, team is building from back**
- GOOD: Fullbacks spread wide to receive; pivot drops slightly to receive; wingers move wider—creates passing options and space
- GOOD: When passing to fullback, fullback immediately has midfielder coming to support in short space
- POOR: All players stand in their normal positions; goalkeeper has limited passing angles
- POOR: When fullback receives, no midfield support nearby; fullback has to dribble or play long

**Scenario C: Team is attacking, opposition fullback is pressing winger**
- GOOD: Winger fakes outside and cuts inside, creating 1-v-1 space; striker moves slightly to be ready if cutback pass available
- GOOD: Winger holds fullback's attention; striker makes diagonal run, pulling centerback out of position, creates space for midfielder
- POOR: Winger just holds ball, no movement; opponent organizes defense easily
- POOR: Striker makes run too early; centerback steps up and marks, closing the gap

## Key coaching points

### Movement Principles
- **Support principle**: Always ensure ball carrier has 2+ passing options within 5-10 meters
- **Width principle**: Spread the opposition by being wide; creates space in center
- **Depth principle**: Have players at different heights (deep, midfield, forward) to give options
- **Variation principle**: Runs should vary (deep, lateral, checking back) so defenders can't anticipate
- **Timing principle**: Runs should be timed with ball movement, not independently

### Drills
- **Possession with movement**: Keep-away with rule that players must move off the ball after passing (not stand still)
- **Run-and-receive**: Practice timed runs where player runs and receives at precise moment
- **Shape maintenance**: Work in a 5-v-5 area where team must maintain width/depth while moving
- **Support angles**: Practice receiving with good angles (on front foot, in direction of play)
- **Movement patterns**: Teach specific patterns (overlaps, underlaps, diagonal runs, checking back)

### Positional Requirements by Role

**Fullbacks:**
- Move wide to receive on wing
- Support winger by advancing behind or alongside
- Adjust depth based on possession phase (higher when attacking, lower when defending)
- Create width through positioning, pulling opposition fullback

**Center-backs:**
- Maintain distance (5-10 meters) from each other
- Step up together when possession is in attacking half
- Drop and tighten when opponent is counter-attacking
- Position to cover gaps (not both on same side of field)

**Defensive Midfielders:**
- Be ready to receive from center-backs
- Create passing angle by moving slightly (not standing flat)
- Provide cover for fullbacks when they advance
- Move deeper when team is in defensive transition

**Box-to-box Midfielders:**
- Constantly moving between positions—supporting deep and arriving in box
- Make runs into box when attacking
- Recover to midfield quickly when defending
- Provide support on both sides of the field

**Attacking Midfielders/Wingers:**
- Move to space between defenders
- Make runs in/out of box (timed with ball movement)
- Provide width by positioning wide
- Check back when needed to create passing angle

**Forwards/Strikers:**
- Occupy defenders even when not receiving
- Make runs to pull defenders out of position
- Time runs to coincide with service
- Create space for midfielders by moving defenders

### Video Analysis
- **Off-ball quality assessment**: During possession, rate each player's positioning (1=poor, 5=excellent) every 5 seconds
- **Run effectiveness**: Did the run create space? Did it result in pass/shot opportunity?
- **Support quality**: How many passing options does ball carrier have at each moment?
- **Shape maintenance**: Does team maintain correct distances/width throughout possession?

### Match Readiness
- Players should understand:
  - What movement is required in their position
  - When to move (not randomly)
  - Where to move (toward space, away from defender, in support)
  - Why they're moving (creating space, supporting, threatening goal)
- Off-ball movement is fitness-intensive; ensure players are fresh enough to execute
