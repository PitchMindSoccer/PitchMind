# Aggressive 1v1 Pressing

## What it is
Aggressive 1v1 pressing is the practice of individual players closing down opponents aggressively in one-on-one contests, designed to force poor decision-making, hurried passing, or turnovers. It emphasizes movement, body position, and timing to limit the opponent's time and space.

## Why it matters (player development)
- **Core defensive skill** across all positions - every player must press effectively
- **Forces decision-making pressure** - reduces time available for quality passes
- **Creates turnovers** - aggressive pressing can win possession in dangerous areas
- **Team organization** - individual 1v1 pressing creates collective defensive structure
- **Confidence building** - developing comfort in closing down opponents builds players' defensive confidence

## Universal principles (applies to ALL positions)

### 1. **Approach & Body Position**
- Approach on a curved path (not straight) to force the opponent wide
- Body position: shoulder-to-shoulder, slightly in front of the opponent
- Head up to read the next pass, not just focused on the ball carrier
- Stay on balls of feet, ready to adjust direction quickly

### 2. **Closing Speed & Timing**
- Initial closing is aggressive but controlled - not reckless
- 2-3 strides to close the gap, then adjust stance
- Anticipate movement - don't just react to what the opponent does
- Use pressure to force the opponent into a predetermined passing lane

### 3. **Space Management**
- Define "closing zones" - where you'll press aggressively vs. dropping
- Deny the opponent's preferred passing option
- Force them to use their weaker foot or less preferred direction
- Position to intercept or block the likely pass

### 4. **Commitment & Support**
- Once you commit to pressing, stay with it - no half measures
- Secondary pressers must adjust to support the primary presser
- Form "pressure triangles" - two defending players working together
- Pressing is a collective action, not individual heroics

## Position context (how it differs by role)

### Forward
- **Press triggers**: Immediately when possession is lost, especially in midfield
- **Movement**: Quick, aggressive approaches to close space
- **Secondary role**: Link with midfielders to form pressing unit

### Midfielder
- **Press triggers**: Based on match situation (pressing triggers, not always immediate)
- **Positioning**: Scan and react - cut off passing lanes
- **Balance**: Know when to press and when to cover behind

### Defender
- **Press triggers**: Only press if it fits defensive shape (usually defensive 3rd)
- **Movement**: Controlled, rarely commit aggressively to 1v1
- **Support**: Cover for pressing teammates, protect space behind

### Fullback
- **Press triggers**: Relevant when isolated 1v1 with wingers
- **Unique aspect**: Often pressing while backwards (covering the wing)
- **Recovery**: Know your back post responsibility

## What GOOD looks like

### Good 1v1 Pressing Execution
- Player closes on curved path, not straight line
- Body position is side-on, not front-on (can adjust both directions)
- Opponent is forced wide or into a difficult passing situation
- Supporting defenders are positioned to intercept the likely pass
- Presser remains on feet (doesn't dive/slide recklessly)
- Match outcome: Ball carrier loses possession or makes poor pass

### Example: Aggressive 1v1 in midfield
- Midfielder reads a loose pass is coming toward opponent
- Closes aggressively on curved path, forcing opponent to left foot
- Opponent panics, plays hospital pass back to defender
- Secondary presser intercepts the return pass
- **Result:** Pressing won possession in attacking area

## What POOR looks like

### Poor 1v1 Pressing Execution
- Player closes in straight line, opponent simply moves right/left
- Body position is front-on with weight on heels (slow to react)
- Presser slides/dives and is beaten easily
- Presser commits, but no support - creates space elsewhere
- Opponent has time and space to pick a quality pass
- Match outcome: Easy pass played through or around presser

### Common Mistakes
- **Pressing too late**: Opponent already has time to pass
- **No body angle**: Straight approach allows easy evade
- **Diving/sliding**: Leaves space behind, can't recover
- **Isolated pressing**: Pressing without team support
- **Inconsistency**: Pressing sometimes but not others (confuses teammates)

## Key coaching points

### 1. **Curved Approach Pattern**
- Teach players to curve runs, not straight lines
- Narrows opponent's passing options (forces one direction)
- Allows presser to adjust based on opponent movement

### 2. **Read the Game First**
- Head up while approaching - know where the pass is likely going
- Anticipate vs. react - get there before opponent receives
- Pressure point is created before the ball arrives

### 3. **Side-On Body Position**
- Feet should be on a line perpendicular to the goal (not facing goal)
- Allows quick adjustment both left and right
- Can apply pressure without overcommitting

### 4. **Stay on Feet**
- Sliding/diving is a last resort, not primary technique
- Stay on feet to recover if beaten
- Can apply continuous pressure, not one-off action

### 5. **Support Your Presser**
- 1v1 pressing works best as 2v2
- Second defender positions to intercept likely pass
- Create "pressure pocket" around ball carrier

### 6. **Pressing Triggers (When to Press)**
- **Immediate press**: Ball just lost (counter-press)
- **Positional press**: Set up to press in specific zones
- **Reading the game**: Press when opponent is in weak position
- **Match context**: Press more when ahead (protect lead), less when behind

## Teaching progression

### Unopposed Stage (Foundation)
- Players practice approach patterns (curved runs)
- Work on body positioning and stance
- No opponent resistance

### Passive Opposition (Intermediate)
- Opponent has ball but moves slowly
- Presser practices timing and adjustment
- Opponent doesn't actively resist

### Active Opposition (Advanced)
- Opponent tries to evade or pass
- Presser must commit timing and intensity
- Create 1v1 situation

### Small-Sided Games (Game-Realistic)
- Multiple 1v1 contests simultaneously
- Must read game, anticipate, support teammates
- Match-realistic decision-making
