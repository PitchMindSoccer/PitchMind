# CAM Fundamentals — Attacking Midfielder (Playmaker, Number 10)
*Leo Session 012 — Deep Dive into Modern Attacking Midfielder Play*

---

## Position Overview: The Creative Orchestrator

The Attacking Midfielder (CAM)—also called the Playmaker, Number 10, or Shadow Striker—is the **most creative and dangerous passing position on the field**. Unlike the CM (balanced) or CDM (defensive), the CAM is **purely attacking in intent**. Their primary job is to create goal-scoring chances for others, with some direct goal contribution expected.

### Three Modern CAM Archetypes

**1. The Classic Playmaker (10 In The Hole)**
- Receives between the lines (between opposition midfield and defensive line)
- Looks to **split the defense** with through-balls or penetrating passes
- Low defensive work rate; relies on midfield to cover for them
- Exemplars: De Bruyne (Manchester City), Mesut Özil (at his best), Isco (Real Madrid)
- Radar emphasis: Passing accuracy, chance creation, decision-making
- Risk: Defensively vulnerable; can be isolated if the midfield isn't covering

**2. The Direct Creator (Ball-Carrier Into Final Third)**
- Dribbles forward with the ball, creating space for others or finishing themselves
- Often the starting point of a chance (dribbles around 1-2 defenders, then releases a pass)
- Higher workload than classic playmaker; expected to contribute defensively
- Exemplars: Neymar (when playing as CAM), Müller, Grealish (when operating as an attacking mid)
- Radar emphasis: Dribbling, decision-making, composure under pressure
- Strength: Creates chaos; difficult to mark. Weakness: Can be predictable (always tries to dribble through)

**3. The Free-Roaming Facilitator (Position-Fluid)**
- Moves across the field—sometimes left, sometimes right, sometimes behind the striker
- Finds pockets of space and receives the ball in multiple zones
- Can play as a 10, a left-sided 8, or even a right-sided 8 depending on the moment
- Exemplars: Vinícius Júnior (when drifting centrally), Timo Werner (when playing closer to goal)
- Radar emphasis: Movement intelligence, awareness, decision-making
- Challenge: Difficult to assess from video (no fixed position = inconsistent radar metrics)

## Key Responsibilities

### Possession Phase (60-70% of match)

**Chance Creation (The Primary Job):**
- Receives the ball in advanced positions (within 20-30 yards of opposition goal)
- Decides: **Shoot? Pass? Dribble?**
- If passing: Must create a **clear-cut chance** (1v1 with goalkeeper, tap-in, open net)
- Creates chances through:
  - Through-balls (splitting the defense)
  - Crosses (from the center to the wing)
  - Cut-back passes (from near the byline to the penalty area)
  - Low crosses (along the ground to a teammate's run)
  - Chip passes (over a defender to a teammate's run)

**Advanced Ball Carrying:**
- Dribbles forward into spaces (unlike CM, the CAM's dribbling is often **end-goal oriented**—meant to create a chance)
- Uses body feints, acceleration, and changes of pace to create shooting/passing space
- Recognizes **when to release the ball** (before running into a dead-end)

**Tempo Orchestration:**
- Controls the **speed of attacks**
- Sometimes slows the play (receiving, holding the ball, attracting defenders) to allow teammates to position
- Sometimes accelerates the attack (one-touch pass that starts a counter-attack)

**Finding Pockets of Space:**
- Moves continuously to find **gaps in the opposition midfield**
- Positions on the **half-turn** (can receive and immediately release forward)
- Operates in the zone 15-25 yards from the opposition goal (the space between CDM and striker)

### Out-of-Possession Phase (30-40% of match)

**Pressing the Opposition (Attacking Pressure):**
- The "first line" of pressing—goes after the opposition's center-backs or fullbacks
- Aims to **force an error** or intercept the pass
- Must decide: Press immediately, or drop and show the passing lane (to the winger)

**Defensive Shape (Limited Role):**
- Less defensive responsibility than CM; often operates as a **floating defender**
- Positions behind the midfield line, ready to intercept passes meant for the striker or attacking fullback
- **Does not usually track back to the fullback line** (that's the winger's job)

**Second-Phase Pressing:**
- If the first press fails, drops back and re-engages when the ball comes forward again
- Looks for **quick turnovers** to restart the attack

**Tracking Opposition Movements:**
- Reads where the opposition's free defender is (usually a trailing CB or fullback)
- Positions to intercept passes meant for runners
- Less systematic than CD defending; more reactive and situational

## Movement Patterns That Define CAM Play

### The Between-The-Lines Reception

**The Setup:**
- Opposition has built up play from their center-backs
- Opposition CDM is in possession
- CAM notices a gap: the opposition's pressing triggers a space between the CDM and the defenders

**The Movement:**
- CAM makes a **forward run** into that gap, perpendicular to the opposition's play direction
- The movement is **subtle**—not a sprint, but a purposeful walk forward, recognizing the moment the opposition CDM's attention is elsewhere

**The Reception:**
- CM or fullback plays a pass into this gap
- CAM receives **with a touch on the half-turn** (hips already open toward the opposition goal)
- Can immediately release a forward pass or dribble toward goal

**Why it's effective:**
- CAM receives with **time and space** (opposition back four is flat, no immediate pressure)
- Opposition midfield can't press immediately (they're oriented the wrong direction)
- Creates a numerical advantage in the attacking zone

### The Dribble-and-Release Sequence

**The Setup:**
- CAM receives the ball in midfield with space ahead
- Opposition has committed 1-2 defenders to pressure the ball-carrier

**The Dribble:**
- Takes 2-3 dribbling touches **into the space**, not toward a specific defender
- The dribbling movement itself **draws defenders inward**
- After 2-3 touches, a teammate (fullback or winger) is now in space on the flank

**The Release:**
- CAM stops dribbling and plays a **crossing pass or cutback** to the now-open fullback/winger
- Fullback/winger has space and time to cross into the box

**Why different from CM dribbling:**
- CM dribbles to open passing lanes for teammates
- CAM dribbles to create **direct attacking threat** (either a chance for themselves or a draw-and-release for a teammate)

### The Floating Movement (Position-Fluid CAM)

**The Setup:**
- Midfielder shifts the play from left wing to right wing (or vice versa)
- Opposition wingers and fullbacks are now compressed on one side

**The Movement:**
- CAM floats to the opposite side (the side that's now open)
- Receives the ball in space on that side
- Can now make a **direct crossing pass** or dribble into the box

**Why it's effective:**
- Opposition marking (designed for a certain formation) breaks down when the ball switches
- CAM receives in space because they've vacated their original position

## Decision-Making Frameworks for CAM Play

### The Receiving Decision Tree (In Advanced Positions)

```
Scenario: CAM receives the ball 20 yards from goal, opposition defenders are 5-10 yards away

├─ **Do I have time to take a touch?**
│  ├─ YES → Scan and decide: shoot, pass, dribble
│  └─ NO → One-touch pass (pre-decided pass)
│
├─ **Is there a clear path to goal?**
│  ├─ YES → Dribble 1-2 touches toward goal (attract defenders)
│  └─ NO → Assess passing options
│
├─ **Can I shoot from here?**
│  ├─ YES (clear sight line, good position) → Assess goalkeeper position
│  │  ├─ Goalkeeper is high → Attempt a chip
│  │  └─ Goalkeeper is positioned normally → Shot on ground or near post
│  └─ NO (blocked, poor angle) → Pass
│
├─ **Passing options:**
│  ├─ Is there a teammate in the box? (1v1 with goalkeeper)
│  │  └─ YES → Through-ball or cut-back
│  ├─ Is there a teammate on the flank with space to cross?
│  │  └─ YES → Pass to the flank (switch or simple pass)
│  └─ Is there a teammate making a run?
│     └─ YES → Release pass into the run's path
│
└─ **If no clear option:**
   └─ Recycle possession (backward pass, or dribble to reset position)
```

### The Press Decision (Out of Possession)

Unlike the CM, the CAM's pressing decision is **simpler**:

**Decision:** Do I press the opposition's center-back/fullback, or do I stay back?

**Factors:**
1. **Distance to the ball-carrier:** Can I reach them in 2-3 steps?
2. **Backup support:** Is a winger nearby to provide second pressure?
3. **Risk:** If I press and miss, am I exposing the striker (will they be 1v3 against center-backs)?

**Elite CAM behavior:** Presses when the opposition is clearly building slowly (center-back is holding the ball for 2+ seconds); drops back if the opposition is moving the ball quickly.

## Technical Requirements for CAM Play

### Passing Types & Execution

**1. Through-Ball (10-25 yards)**
- Purpose: **Split the defense** and create a 1v1 with goalkeeper
- Execution: Weight is critical—too soft and the defender intercepts; too hard and it goes out of play or reaches the goalkeeper
- Timing: Must be played when the striker's run and the pass *synchronize* (run starts, pass is played simultaneously)
- Recognition: Identifying when a striker is making a run (not jogging)

**2. Cut-Back Pass (5-15 yards, lateral)**
- Purpose: From a wide position (near the byline), play the ball back to a teammate making a late run into the box
- Execution: Low pass along the ground, weighted so the receiver can take one touch and shoot
- Key difference: The receiver is behind the CAM (CAM is wide, receiver is coming from deeper)

**3. Low Cross (15-25 yards)**
- Purpose: From a wide or central position, deliver the ball to the penalty area at knee height
- Execution: Curved pass (if crossing from the wing), or direct pass (if in the center)
- Recognition: Where are the teammates in the box? Is there a near-post runner? A far-post runner? A central runner?

**4. Chip Pass (10-20 yards)**
- Purpose: Loft the ball over a defender's head to a teammate
- Execution: Toed pass (top of the foot), gentle strike; ball arcs over the defender
- Usage: When the direct passing lane is blocked, but there's space above the defender

**5. Penetrating Pass (Inside Foot To The Foot)**
- Purpose: Play a pass through congestion into a teammate's feet (not into space)
- Execution: Weight is lower than a through-ball; receiver will take a touch and turn
- Recognition: Identifying when a teammate needs the ball at their feet (for a quick one-two or turn-and-shoot)

**6. One-Touch Pass (5-30 yards)**
- Purpose: Play the ball immediately upon receiving (no touch)
- Execution: Depends on the angle of reception; often a side-foot pass or flick
- High-difficulty: Executed under pressure or with limited time

### Shooting Technique

**Types of shots CAMs take:**

| Shot Type | Setup | Execution |
|---|---|---|
| **Near-post strike** | Receives 12-18 yards out, goalkeeper is high | Instep drive with power (bottom corner) |
| **Far-post placement** | Receives 12-18 yards out, goalkeeper is positioned centrally | Finesse shot (less power, more placement) with the outside foot |
| **Chip** | Goalkeeper is high; space between goalkeeper and goal line | Toed pass technique; gentle strike |
| **Volley** | Ball comes from a cross or pass; CAM is in the penalty area | Strike with the inside or instep of foot |
| **Driven pass that becomes a shot** | Ball is rolling across the face of the goal | Low strike, aiming to hit a teammate in the box (or goal if clear) |

**Leo scoring note:** CAMs score lower on "shooting accuracy" than strikers because they shoot less frequently and from more difficult positions. But a CAM who converts 15-20% of their shots (from difficult positions) is elite.

### Dribbling with Intent

**Type 1: Dribbling to Create Space (1-2 touches)**
- Receives the ball with a defender close
- Takes 1-2 dribbles to **shift the defender's position** slightly
- Immediately releases a pass to a now-open teammate

**Type 2: Dribbling to Attack (2-4 touches)**
- Receives with space ahead
- Dribbles toward the opposition box
- Either shoots or draws a defender before releasing

**Type 3: Dribbling to Reset (1-3 touches)**
- Receives with limited forward options
- Dribbles slightly sideways or backwards to reset position and create new passing angles
- Eventually plays the ball forward once a new passing lane opens

## Tactical Awareness: Reading Opposition & Creating Advantages

### Pre-Pass Positioning Reading

Elite CAMs read the **opposition's formation** before the ball reaches them:

**Observations:**
1. **Opposition fullback positioning:** Are they high (pressed forward) or deep? (Tells you if there's space on the flank)
2. **Opposition midfield spacing:** Are they compact or spread? (Tells you where the gaps are)
3. **Opposition striker's body language:** Are they making a run or staying central? (Tells you what pass type is needed)
4. **Opposition center-back attention:** Who are they marking? Is there a free defender? (Tells you if there's a through-ball or if you need to dribble)

**Action:**
- If opposition fullback is high → "There's space behind them; I can play a through-ball"
- If opposition midfield is spread → "I have time and space; I can dribble forward"
- If opposition striker is making a run → "I need to play a through-ball that matches their run timing"

### Recognizing When to Create vs. When to Recycle

**Create (Push the Attack):** When opposition has committed many players forward, leaving space to exploit
**Recycle (Possession):** When opposition is compact and a direct attack would lose the ball

**The decision is made in **0.5 seconds** after receiving the ball.**

## Radar Axes for CAM Scoring

LEO must evaluate CAMs across **seven key dimensions:**

### 1. **Chance Creation Quality**
- How many **clear-cut chances** does the CAM create per match?
- Quality of passes: Are the passes leading to shots, or are they just "putting teammates in space"?
- Assists + key passes (passes that lead directly to a shot)

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ Creates a 1v1 with goalkeeper (clear-cut chance)
- ✅ Provides a pass leading directly to a shot
- ❌ Passes that put teammate in space but not in a shooting opportunity
- ❌ Turnover that leads to opposition chance (opposite of creating)

### 2. **Dribbling & Ball Carrying (In Attacking Zones)**
- How often does the CAM dribble forward into attacking zones (final third)?
- Dribbling completion % (successful dribbles)
- Dribbling that leads to a pass or shot (purposeful vs. aimless)

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ Dribble 3-4 touches into the opposition box
- ✅ Dribble that attracts 2+ defenders (creating space for teammates)
- ❌ Dribble that ends in a turnover or runs into a dead-end
- ✅ Dribble that leads to a shot or crossing pass

### 3. **Shooting Quality & Efficiency**
- Shot accuracy (% of shots on target)
- Shot difficulty assessment (is the CAM shooting from high-probability or low-probability positions?)
- Expected goals (xG) contribution

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ Shot from 12-18 yards, on target
- ✅ Finishes a chance when it's clear-cut
- ❌ Shot from a difficult angle (unlikely to score)
- ❌ Shoots when passing would have been better

### 4. **Decision-Making Under Pressure**
- Pass completion % under pressure
- Do CAMs turn the ball over when pressed, or do they find a safe pass?
- How many times does the CAM make a **correct decision** when multiple options are available?

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ Stays calm under pressure; plays a simple pass
- ❌ Turnover from a forced pass or poor decision
- ✅ Recognizes when to recycle possession (doesn't force a risky pass)
- ❌ Panic pass that leads to opposition counter-attack

### 5. **Positioning & Off-Ball Movement**
- Is the CAM in the **right position** to receive the ball when the team is building?
- Does the CAM make runs to find space, or do they wait statically?
- How many times does the CAM receive in advanced positions (between the lines)?

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ Makes a forward run into the gap; receives in space
- ❌ Stays static; waits for the ball instead of moving
- ✅ Positions on the half-turn (can release forward immediately)
- ❌ Receives with their back to goal; needs an extra touch to turn

### 6. **Pressing & Defensive Contribution (Limited Role)**
- How many times does the CAM press the opposition and force an error?
- Does the CAM press at the right time (when possession is loose) or at the wrong time (when opposition is secure)?
- Recovery speed after a failed press

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ Presses and forces a turnover
- ❌ Presses and gets beaten (opposition plays through them)
- ✅ Drops back quickly when the first press fails
- ❌ Stranded forward after a failed press

### 7. **Composure & Clutch Performance**
- How does the CAM perform in **high-pressure moments** (last 15 minutes, close score, elimination matches)?
- Decision-making in crucial moments (do they take risks when risks are needed, or do they play safe?)
- Recovery from mistakes (do they immediately hunt the ball back, or do they jog back?)

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ Makes a crucial pass or chance creation in the last minutes
- ✅ Stays calm in high-pressure situations
- ❌ Makes an error that leads to a goal against in crucial moments
- ✅ Immediate recovery after a mistake

## Sub-Category Scoring Relevance for CAM

**EXECUTION Sub-Categories (Technical):**
- **Shooting:** 🟢 High — CAM shoots regularly; shooting accuracy is critical
- **Passing/Distribution:** 🟢 High — The primary job is creating chances through passing
- **Dribbling:** 🟡 Moderate — Dribbles in attacking zones, but less than a winger
- **First Touch:** 🟡 Moderate — Receives often in tight spaces; first touch quality matters

**PROBABILITY Sub-Categories (Soccer IQ):**
- **Decision Making:** 🟢 Highest — Every action is a decision: shoot? Pass? Dribble? Timing is critical
- **Awareness:** 🟢 High — Must scan constantly; needs to know where space and teammates are
- **Reading the Game:** 🟡 Moderate — Reads opposition defense (where are the gaps?), but less about transitions than CM
- **Positioning:** 🟡 Moderate — Must position to receive, but less about defensive shape than CM
- **Concentration/Focus:** 🟡 Moderate — Mistakes in the attacking third can lead to counter-attacks, but less focus-intensive than a goalkeeper

## Common CAM Mistakes & Elite Corrections

| Mistake | Why It Happens | Elite Correction | Leo Scoring Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Shooting when passing is better** | Greedy; always thinks they can finish | Trust the teammate; a tap-in for a teammate is better than a difficult shot | Difficult shot: ❌ -1. Key pass: ✅ +2 |
| **Not recognizing an open teammate** | Tunnel vision; focused on their own options | Scan before receiving; know the field before the ball arrives | Difficult pass attempt: ❌ -1. Simple pass to open teammate: ✅ +1 |
| **Playing a pass to a teammate's back foot** | Not observing body orientation | Look at body shape; pass to the outside foot (enables forward play) | Teammate can't progress: ❌ -1. Teammate can shoot/progress: ✅ +1 |
| **Running into a dead-end (dribbling with no exit)** | Over-dribbles; loses awareness | Dribble with intention—know who you're passing to before you dribble | Ball lost: ❌ -2. Dribble and pass: ✅ +1 |
| **Pressing when opposition is secure** | Eagerness; wants to win the ball back | Press only when possession is loose; otherwise, drop back and organize defense | Beaten by the dribble; opposition counter: ❌ -2. Smart press: ✅ +1 |
| **Losing focus defensively (gap opens in midfield)** | Focused on attacking; forgets defensive responsibility | Defense is a shared job; even attackers must cover gaps | Opposition scores from midfield gap: ❌ -3. Covers gap: ✅ +1 |
| **Not making a forward run when movement is needed** | Laziness or fatigue; waiting for the ball | Movement is half the job; make runs constantly to provide passing options | Ball is played backward because no options: ❌ -1. Run opens space: ✅ +2 |
| **Panic pass under pressure** | Feels pressed; tries to force a pass | Recycle possession; play sideways or backward if forward is blocked | Turnover: ❌ -2. Calm pass: ✅ +1 |

## Age-Group Considerations for CAM Scoring

| Age Group | Development Stage | Leo Scoring Adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| **U10–U12** | Learning to **recognize space**; passing accuracy is low | Reward runs and positioning; lower expectations for chance creation; reward simple passes that reach a teammate |
| **U14–U16** | Starting to **read opposition** and identify passing lanes; decision-making is inconsistent | Expect some chance creation; penalize for poor decision-making; reward for splitting the defense |
| **U18–College** | Full responsibility for chance creation; reading game is expected; decision-making under pressure is critical | High penalties for missed chances; reward for assists and key passes; expect intelligent pressing |
| **Professional** | Every action is purposeful; chance creation is expected; decision-making is elite-level | Elite scoring: reward for high-quality chances created; harsh penalties for turnovers in dangerous areas; expect optimal decision-making |

## Elite CAM Types & Their Radar Profiles

**1. The Pure Creator** (De Bruyne, Tadic, Isco)
- Radar emphasis: Chance creation, passing accuracy, decision-making
- Radar weakness: Shooting (prefers to pass)
- Scoring approach: High marks for key passes and assists; lower marks for direct goal contribution

**2. The Direct Scorer** (Müller, Greenwood, Havertz when playing CAM)
- Radar emphasis: Shooting, positioning, composure
- Radar weakness: Playmaking (prefers to shoot)
- Scoring approach: High marks for shots on target; moderate marks for chance creation

**3. The Dribbler-Creator** (Neymar, Grealish, Vinícius)
- Radar emphasis: Dribbling, ball carrying, decision-making
- Radar weakness: Positioning discipline (roams too much)
- Scoring approach: High marks for dribbling and direct action; penalties for being out of position

**4. The Intelligent Facilitator** (Ziyech, David Silva, Bernardo Silva)
- Radar emphasis: Awareness, reading the game, composure
- Radar weakness: Pressing (often stays back)
- Scoring approach: High marks for off-ball movement and intelligent positioning; penalties for defensive lapses

**5. The Inverted Winger Playing CAM** (ESR as CAM, Buendia as CAM)
- Radar emphasis: Dribbling, playmaking, direct action
- Radar weakness: Defensive responsibility (forgets to press or cover)
- Scoring approach: High marks for dribbling and creativity; penalties for defensive gaps

- "Look before you touch—know where your next action is"

- "If pressed, recycle; don't force a turnover"

**Pressing:**
- "Press only when you can win the ball—don't press the secure pass"
- "If you press and miss, drop back immediately"

## Training Exercises for CAM Development

### Exercise 1: Between-The-Lines Reception & Release
**Objective:** Develop positioning in the gap between opposition midfield and defense

**Setup:**
- 30x25 yard area
- 3v3 (2 CMs or fullbacks + 1 ST building play; 1 CAM receiving; 2 defenders)
- Team in possession must play a pass to the CAM in the gap
- CAM receives and must make a forward pass or shot within 2 seconds

**Progression:**
- Increase pressure (add a defender pressing the CAM immediately)
- Reduce space (smaller area)
- Add a requirement (must create a 1v1 with goalkeeper)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- How often is the CAM in the correct position to receive?
- How quickly does the CAM release the ball after receiving?
- Quality of the pass/shot (does it create a chance)?

---

### Exercise 2: Chance Creation Rondo
**Objective:** Develop chance-creating passing under pressure

**Setup:**
- 25x20 yard area
- 4v2 (4 attackers keeping possession, 2 defenders pressing)
- The 4 attackers must make 10 consecutive passes, with the CAM creating a **clear-cut chance** (pass that leads to a shot)
- After the chance is created, reset and repeat

**Progression:**
- Reduce the area (3v2, then 2v2 in smaller space)
- Add a target (must reach the "goal line" at the end)
- Add a shot requirement (chance must result in a shot on goal)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- How many touches before creating a chance? (Efficiency)
- Quality of the chance (1v1, tap-in, etc.)
- Decision-making (did they shoot when they should have passed, or vice versa?)

---

### Exercise 3: Dribble-and-Release Drill
**Objective:** Develop dribbling with purpose (not end-goal dribbling)

**Setup:**
- 35x25 yard area
- CAM receives in midfield with space ahead and a defender nearby
- CAM dribbles forward 2-3 touches, attracting the defender
- As the defender closes in, a teammate (winger or fullback) opens up on the flank
- CAM releases a crossing pass

**Progression:**
- Add more defenders (2-3 pressers)
- Add a goalkeeper and attacking team (make it game-like)
- Reduce time (5 seconds to complete the sequence)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- Does the CAM dribble with purpose (not just running with the ball)?
- Does the dribble attract defenders, creating space for teammates?
- Is the release pass weighted correctly?

---

### Exercise 4: Shooting From Tight Angles
**Objective:** Develop shooting quality from difficult positions

**Setup:**
- 18-yard box
- CAM receives the ball in the box from various angles (12 yards out, from the wing, from the byline)
- Must shoot within 2 touches
- Goalkeeper provides realistic opposition

**Progression:**
- Add defenders (closing down the CAM)
- Add realistic passes (not stationary receptions)
- Add movement (CAM must make a run to receive, not wait for the pass)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- Shot accuracy from different angles
- Decision-making (when to shoot vs. when to pass)
- Technique (where does the ball go?)

---

### Exercise 5: Decision-Making Rondo (Shoot/Pass/Dribble)
**Objective:** Develop decision-making in real-time game scenarios

**Setup:**
- 30x25 yard area
- 4v2 (4 attackers, 2 defenders, goalkeeper)
- Attackers are building play and must eventually reach the CAM
- CAM receives with multiple options: a teammate in the box (pass), a shooting opportunity (shoot), or space to dribble
- All three options are viable; CAM must choose the best one

**Progression:**
- Change the setup so different options are available each round
- Add time pressure (must decide in 2-3 seconds)
- Add realistic positions (CAM receives from different angles)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- Decision accuracy (did they make the right choice?)
- Shot selection (shoot from good positions, pass from bad positions?)
- Pass quality (does the pass create a chance?)

---

### Exercise 6: Pressing Under Control
**Objective:** Develop intelligent pressing (not excessive pressing)

**Setup:**
- 50x40 yard area (half field)
- 3v3 (attackers building play; defenders including CAM trying to win the ball back)
- Attackers must make 5 passes in the build-up zone; after 5 passes, the CAM can press
- If the CAM presses before 5 passes, it's a penalty (opposition gets a free pass forward)

**Progression:**
- Change the timing of when pressing is allowed
- Add realism (no whistle; CAM must read when possession is loose)
- Add consequences (if CAM loses a pressing duel, opposition scores)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- Does the CAM press at the right time (when possession is loose)?
- What's the outcome of the press (win the ball, or beaten)?
- Recovery speed after a failed press

---

### Exercise 7: Movement Into Space (Off-Ball)
**Objective:** Develop runs and positioning to create passing options

**Setup:**
- 50x40 yard area (half field)
- 5v3 (5 attackers, 3 defenders)
- Attackers must make 10 consecutive passes
- CAM is one of the 5 attackers; their job is to make intelligent runs to create passing options
- Every time a run creates a passing option (whether the pass is made or not), it's a success

**Progression:**
- Increase defenders (5v4, then 5v5)
- Add a requirement (each of the 5 attackers must receive the ball at least once)
- Add a goal (complete 10 passes while the CAM has created 3+ passing options through movement)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- Frequency of runs (is the CAM constantly moving, or static?)
- Quality of runs (do they create space for teammates?)
- Timing of runs (run at the right moment, or too early/late?)

## Curious Questions for Leo to Investigate Further
