# CM Fundamentals — Central Midfielder (Box-to-Box, Number 8)
*Leo Session 012 — Deep Dive into Modern Central Midfielder Play*

## Position Overview: The Tactical Chameleon

The Central Midfielder (CM)—also called Box-to-Box Midfielder or Number 8—is soccer's most versatile position. Unlike the CDM (defensive stability) or CAM (attacking creativity), the CM is **both offensive and defensive in equal measure**, demanding a different decision-making framework than either.

### Three Modern CM Archetypes

**1. The Energetic Box-to-Box (Classic)**
- High work rate, consistent involvement in build-up and defense
- Covers ground defensively, then drives forward to support attacks
- Exemplars: Steven Gerrard (Liverpool), Yaya Touré (Manchester City), Javi Martínez (Bayern)
- Radar emphasis: Transition speed, positional discipline, ball progression
- Risk: Can become "jack of all trades, master of none" if not careful about positioning specifics

**2. The Positional Conductor (Modern 2020s)**
- Less ground-covering, more **spatial intelligence and passing accuracy**
- Controls tempo and rhythm of the team
- Uses half-space occupation and third-man runs to circulate play
- Exemplars: Rodri (Manchester City), Bruno Fernandes (Manchester United), Pedri (Barcelona)
- Radar emphasis: Reading the game, decision-making under pressure, passing accuracy
- Strength: Can unlock tight defenses through positioning and timing; weaker at pure defensive recovery

**3. The Press-Resistant Progressor**
- Thrives under heavy pressure; comfortable receiving in tight spaces
- Drives forward into midfield vacancies created by pressing
- Turns defense into attack quickly
- Exemplars: Kai Havertz (Arsenal), Mats Weigl (Portugal, various clubs)
- Radar emphasis: Press resistance, transition speed, composure in crowded spaces
- Challenge: Often caught out positionally if over-committing to ball-winning

## Key Responsibilities

### Possession Phase (55-60% of match)

**Build-Up & Circulation:**
- Receives from center-backs and fullbacks as a **passing outlet**
- Decides: Hold and turn? Pass sideways to RB/LB? Pass back to CB for reset? Ping to striker?
- Creates **tempo control**—when to slow, when to accelerate progression
- Third-man principle: Often the "third" in CB-to-CM-to-striker passing sequences, timing the run and receiving with back to goal or half-turned for quick release

**Progression into the Final Third:**
- Recognizes when opposition midfield is stretched (fullbacks advanced, strikers isolated) and **drives forward**
- Uses half-space occupation: positions between the winger's lane and striker's lane to receive and turn downfield
- Creates **space for fullbacks and wingers** by pulling defenders inward
- Decides: Dribble into space? Release a through-ball? Play a quick one-two? Delay for supporting runners?

**Offensive Support:**
- Timing of runs into the box or attacking third—not too early (offside), not too late (loses momentum)
- Creating **overload situations** in midfield or attacking zones
- Second-phase involvement after initial pass: positioning for lay-offs, flick-ons, rebounds

**Tempo & Rhythm Control:**
- Controls the **speed of play** rather than just executing passes
- Knows when to play one-touch vs. take a touch (creating a passing lane)
- Often the "quarterback" of the team—decides which direction the ball moves next

### Out-of-Possession Phase (40-45% of match)

**Transition Defense (0-3 seconds after losing ball):**
- Immediate press on the ball-carrier (if close) or adjacent midfielder
- Prevents the first pass from reaching a dangerous area
- Must decide: **Press the ball, or drop to cover space?**—depends on how deep the turnover occurred

**Defensive Shape Maintenance:**
- Stays in a "reactive" position—neither too far forward (exposes defense) nor too deep (cedes midfield control)
- Covers defensive gaps created by fullbacks pressing or center-backs stepping up
- Provides a **second line of defense** between CDM and attacking players

**Interception & Reading:**
- Reads **opposition movement before the pass is made**
- Scans shoulders to identify striker's runs, fullback movements, attacking midfielder's drift
- Intercepts passes meant for the attacking third by positioning in passing lanes
- Different from CDM: the CM intercepts *offensive progression*, not just holding shape

**Pressing vs. Covering Decision:**
- Against ball-carrying midfielder: Should I press, or let CDM handle it and cover space?
- Against inverted fullback (fullback carrying into midfield): Press or cover the winger's space?
- The **timing and positioning** of this decision is what separates elite CMs from average ones

**Second-Ball Contests:**
- Positions near tackles and challenges to win secondary possessions
- Thinks one step ahead: "If this challenge goes 50-50, where does the ball go?"

## Movement Patterns That Define CM Play

### The Halfspace Dance

The **half-space** (zone between the winger's lane and the central striker's lane) is the CM's office.

**What happens in the half-space:**
1. CM drops slightly from forward position to receive the ball from fullback or center-back
2. CM has **three passing options immediately available**: back (CB), sideways (other FB), forward (space or striker)
3. CM can receive **with inside foot open**, allowing a touch-and-turn or immediate forward pass
4. Opposition can't easily cut off this receiver because the CM is between the winger and striker—neither can cover quickly

**Elite CM behavior:**
- Makes **3-5 touches in the half-space**, circulating the ball while drawing defenders in
- Recognizes when a defender is drawn in, creating space on the opposite flank
- Uses the half-space to **switch play** (long diagonal to opposite fullback or winger)

### Progressive Dribbling Runs

CMs dribble forward differently than fullbacks or wingers:

**When:** After a turnover recovery or when space opens in the midfield (opposition striker pressing forward, fullbacks too wide)

**How:** 
- Takes 2-3 dribbling touches into the midfield vacancy
- Looks to **release a pass before the run ends** (doesn't carry the ball into a dead-end)
- Example: Dribbles through the middle, draws the opposition striker, then plays a pass to an overlapping fullback

**Why it's different from CDM dribbling:**
- CDM drives through the center to create immediate attacking opportunities (or to escape pressure)
- CM dribbles to **open passing lanes** for teammates

### The Delayed Run (Box-to-Box Movement)

**Pattern:** CM receives a pass, holds the ball briefly, then makes a **late run into the penalty area or attacking third** as a support option.

**When it's used:**
- Striker has the ball on the wing or is isolated
- CM recognizes: "The striker needs support; I'll peel off my defensive position"
- Makes a timed run to the **back stick or far post** for a potential tap-in

**Why it works:**
- Defenders are focused on the ball-carrier (striker or fullback)
- CM is "free" because they started in a defensive position
- Creates a 2v1 situation in the box

## Decision-Making Frameworks for CM Play

### Receiving Under Pressure: The CM's Core Challenge

CM receives in **three scenarios:**

| Scenario | Pressure Level | Touch Type | Pass Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Buildout** | Low (CDM nearby, space ahead) | Heavy touch (taking a step or two) | Forward pass, turn and progress, or sideways circulation |
| **Midfield Transition** | **Medium-High** (opposition pressing, space limited) | **One-touch or immediate touch** | Lateral pass (other CM), backward pass (CDM/CB), or find a free runner |
| **Under Attack** | Very high (multiple pressers, space compressed) | **Pre-touch** (know your pass before receiving) | Immediate pass; no dribbling |

**Elite CM signature:** Receives in medium-pressure situations and **progresses the ball** (doesn't just sideways pass). This is where position-specific intelligence shows up—weaker CMs lose the ball or pass backward; elite CMs recognize a fraction-of-a-second window to pass forward.

### The Press/Cover Decision Tree

```
Scenario: Opposition midfielder is running toward the ball-carrier

├─ Is the CDM in position to press?
│  └─ YES → I cover space (drop deeper)
│  └─ NO → I press the midfielder
│
├─ Is the ball-carrier about to release the ball?
│  └─ YES → I position in the passing lane (interception)
│  └─ NO → I close distance to the ball-carrier
│
├─ Am I exposed (far from CB coverage)?
│  └─ YES → I stay compact; call for CDM to press
│  └─ NO → I can afford to press aggressively
```

### The Forward Run Decision

**When to run into the attacking third:**
1. **Space is available** (opposition CDM is isolated, fullbacks advanced, strikers isolated)
2. **A teammate has the ball** and can see me (in peripheral vision, call-outs)
3. **The ball-carrier's pass quality is good** (they're not under pressure)

**When NOT to run:**
1. Opposition is pressing the ball-carrier aggressively (requires defensive support)
2. You're the only defensive midfielder shape between the CB and attacking players
3. The ball-carrier is operating on the opposite flank (too far away to receive in time)

## Technical Requirements for CM Play

### Passing Types & Execution

**1. Short Circulation Pass (1-5 yards)**
- Purpose: Maintain possession when pressed; keep the ball moving
- Execution: Inside foot, weight so the receiver can take one touch and release
- Risk: Playing too slow (allows opposition to organize)

**2. Diagonal Pass to Fullback or Winger (8-15 yards)**
- Purpose: Switch play, create width, progress with space
- Execution: Outside foot (curved pass), lifted (clears pressing opponents)
- Requirement: Must lift the ball slightly; flat diagonal passes are easily intercepted

**3. Forward Through-Ball (10-20 yards)**
- Purpose: Unlock the defense via penetrating pass
- Execution: Weight is **critical**—too soft and striker can't reach it; too hard and it's out of play
- Recognition: Strikers' runs, defensive gaps, timing of the release

**4. Long Switch/Raking Ball (30+ yards)**
- Purpose: Change the entire direction of play, opposite flank to opposite flank
- Execution: Curved ball, often with outside foot; must be weighted for a one-touch or first-time finish
- When: Opposition is compressed on one side, opposite flank is open

**5. Backward Pass (5-10 yards)**
- Purpose: Maintain possession, reset, create space
- Execution: Short, low weight; ensures CDM/CB can progress or recycle
- Judgment: Not a weakness if done at the right time; critical at the wrong time (loses momentum)

### First Touch Under Pressure

| Touch Type | When Used | Execution |
|---|---|---|
| **Heavy touch (2-3 steps forward)** | Low pressure, you want to dribble | Sole of foot, creating space |
| **One-touch pass** | High pressure, immediate release | Sole of foot to stabilize, then push pass off |
| **Half-turn touch** | Medium pressure, creating angle | Inside foot, opening hips to new direction |
| **Back-heel or flick** | Very high pressure, teammate nearby | Audacity + teammate awareness (risky!) |

**Elite CM marker:** Takes a heavy touch when there's space, but knows WHEN there's space (requires reading the opposition's pressure before receiving).

### Tackling & Physical Expression

CMs tackle less than CDMs but more than CAMs:

**When:**
- When opposition midfielder has the ball and you're adjacent (1-2 meters)
- When you've failed to intercept a pass and must recover

**How:**
- From a side-on position (not front-facing)—allows follow-up movement
- Shoulder-to-shoulder positioning, timing the tackle for when the player's weight shifts

**Different from CDM tackling:**
- CDM tackles in front of their own box (desperation play, high-intensity)
- CM tackles in midfield (recovery play, lower risk because there's space behind them to regroup)

## Tactical Awareness: Reading the Opposition

### Pre-Ball Movement Reading

Elite CMs read **what will happen next** before the ball is passed:

**Observations:**
1. **Striker positioning:** Is the striker drifting wide (demanding a winger's space)? Or staying central (threatening a direct pass)?
2. **Fullback body language:** Is the fullback ready to overlap or underlap? (Shows you what's about to happen)
3. **Opposition CDM's positioning:** Are they shallow or deep? Are they covering space or are they moving to receive?
4. **Midfield press formation:** Are opposition midfielders staggered or compact? (Tells you where the pass will go)

**Action:**
- If striker is drifting wide → "I need to cover central space"
- If fullback is preparing to overlap → "I should block that lane or be ready to press the pass"
- If opposition CDM is deep → "The striker will receive a long pass; I'll be ready to press from the side"

### Negative Transitions (Losing the Ball)

**Immediate response (0-1 second):**
- Where is the opposition's first threat? (Closest player with the ball)
- Am I the closest defender? (If yes, apply pressure. If no, drop and cover)

**Secondary positioning (1-3 seconds):**
- Recover to a position between the ball and your goal
- Re-establish shape with CDM and center-backs
- Look for interception opportunities on the next pass

### Positive Transitions (Winning the Ball)

**Decision:** Do I play the ball forward immediately, or do I need to circulate first?

**Factors:**
1. **Space ahead:** Is there a striker in-behind? Is there a fullback in space?
2. **Opposition defensive shape:** Are they compact (require a pass through the lines)? Or stretched (can dribble into space)?
3. **Your positioning:** Are you facing forward or backward? (If backward, might need a touch to turn)

**Elite behavior:** CMs who win the ball and immediately recognize the forward pass (without the regulation "take a touch" response) have a huge impact on transition speed.

## Radar Axes for CM Scoring

LEO must evaluate CMs across **seven key dimensions:**

### 1. **Box-to-Box Timing & Positioning**
- Do runs into attacking spaces come at the **right moment**? (Not too early—offside; not too late—useless)
- Recovery to defensive position after attacking runs—are you back in time for the counter?
- Penalty area presence when the ball is on the wing (ready for tap-ins, rebounds)

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ Run starts after ball-carrier has committed, teammate calls made, space confirmed
- ❌ Run is too early (caught offside), or too late (doesn't affect the play)
- ✅ Back in position to cover before the opposition counter
- ❌ Stranded forward when the ball is turned over

### 2. **Positional Discipline & Half-Space Awareness**
- Do you stay in your "zone" (the middle 40 yards of the field, width-wise), or do you roam?
- How often do you occupy the half-space (between winger and striker lanes)?
- Are you providing a passing option without bunching up teammates?

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ Positioned in half-space, opening passing angles
- ❌ Drifting too wide (duplicates winger's space) or too central (overlaps with CDM)
- ✅ Creating 2v1 or 2v2 situations through positioning
- ❌ Leaving gaps in midfield (opposition runs through your zone)

### 3. **Progression & Ball Carrying**
- How often do you **drive forward** into midfield vacancies?
- Pass accuracy when moving (dribbling and passing simultaneously)?
- Release pass quality—do you pass into space or into feet?

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ Drives forward after winning the ball, progresses 10-15 yards
- ❌ Immediately passes sideways even when space is available
- ✅ Passes to a teammate's outside foot (allows forward play)
- ❌ Passes to feet when space is behind the teammate

### 4. **Defensive Reading & Anticipation**
- Do you read opposition movement **before** the pass is made? (Intercepts vs. reacts)
- How many times do you position in a passing lane and get the ball deflected?
- Recovery after a mistake (opposition wins the ball off you)—do you immediately work to win it back or do you jog back?

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ Steps into a passing lane and intercepts
- ❌ Reacts after the pass is made (too late to intercept)
- ✅ Wins the ball back within 2-3 seconds after losing it
- ❌ Ball is played past you with a simple pass over your head

### 5. **Passing Accuracy Under Pressure**
- Pass completion % in the midfield third (higher difficulty than CDM passing)
- Pass quality—how many times do you release a pass that creates an immediate chance?
- Decision-making—when under pressure, do you pass backward or do you find a forward option?

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ 80%+ pass accuracy; forward passes when pressed
- ❌ <70% pass accuracy or consistently passing backward when pressed
- ✅ A pass that splits the defense or creates a 1v1
- ❌ Turnover in the midfield leads to a shot on goal

### 6. **Transition Speed & Tempo Control**
- How quickly do you transition the ball from defense to attack?
- Do you accelerate play when space opens, or do you slow it down unnecessarily?
- How many fast transitions (direct passes leading to a shot or clear chance) do you complete?

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ One-touch pass that starts a counter-attack
- ❌ Receiving in transition but taking 3+ touches before passing
- ✅ Plays a quick pass when there's space, but slows tempo when it's needed
- ❌ Always plays at max speed, leading to inaccurate passes

### 7. **Composure & Decision-Making Under Pressure**
- How many times are you **dispossessed** in dangerous areas (midfield 40-yard line)?
- Do you make panic passes or do you stay calm?
- Recovery after a poor decision—do you immediately press for the ball back?

**Scoring markers:**
- ✅ Stays calm; makes a simple pass when under pressure
- ❌ Turnover from a rushed or poor decision
- ✅ Immediate press to win the ball back
- ❌ Jogging back after a mistake; lets opposition carry the ball

## Sub-Category Scoring Relevance for CM

**EXECUTION Sub-Categories (Technical):**
- **Distribution:** 🟢 High — CM passing is complex (must progress while maintaining accuracy)
- **Dribbling:** 🟡 Moderate — Carries the ball less than a winger, more than a CDM
- **First Touch:** 🟡 Moderate — Receives in medium pressure; first touch quality matters
- **Tackling:** 🟡 Moderate — Tackles less than CDM, but must recover decisively

**PROBABILITY Sub-Categories (Soccer IQ):**
- **Decision Making:** 🟢 Highest — Box-to-box timing, forward run decisions, press/cover decisions are critical
- **Positioning:** 🟢 Highest — Half-space awareness, defensive shape maintenance
- **Awareness:** 🟢 High — Must scan the field constantly; reads opposition movement pre-pass
- **Reading the Game:** 🟢 High — Recognizes transitions, open space, and pressing cues
- **Concentration/Focus:** 🟡 Moderate — Mistakes in midfield can lead to counter-attacks, but less "focus" intensive than a goalkeeper

## Common CM Mistakes & Elite Corrections

| Mistake | Why It Happens | Elite Correction | Leo Scoring Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Running too early into the box** | Eager to attack; loses patience | Wait for the ball-carrier to commit to a pass first | Caught offside: ❌ -2. Timed run: ✅ +2 |
| **Bunching up with CDM (no half-space)** | Safety; sticks to midfield density | Deliberately move 3-5 yards wider; create **separation** | No passing angle: ❌ -1. Opens passing lanes: ✅ +2 |
| **Receiving and immediately passing back** | Fear of losing the ball; no confidence in the next action | Scan before receiving; have a target in mind | Keeps possession but doesn't progress: ❌ -1. Progressive pass: ✅ +2 |
| **Not pressing when the opposition plays through the middle** | Sitting too deep; fear of leaving space | Read the pass trajectory; step forward to intercept | Reaction: ❌ -1. Anticipation: ✅ +2 |
| **Dribbling into a dead-end (no passing option)** | Carries the ball too far; loses awareness of teammates | Dribble with the intention to **release a pass**; know the target before you dribble | Ball lost: ❌ -2. Dribble and pass: ✅ +2 |
| **Losing concentration defensively after attacking** | Forgets to get back; too focused on the attack | Transition mentality: attack, but always have a defensive "reset" position | Stranded forward during counter: ❌ -2. Back in position: ✅ +2 |
| **Passing to the striker's back heel instead of their forward foot** | Poor observation; not reading the striker's body shape | Look at the striker's body orientation before passing; pass to their **outside foot** for forward play | Striker can't progress: ❌ -1. Creates space: ✅ +1 |
| **Panic pass under pressure** | Pressure gets to you; rushed decision | Take a breath; play a simple pass (backward or sideways) rather than forcing a risky forward pass | Turnover: ❌ -2. Calm pass: ✅ +1 |

## Age-Group Considerations for CM Scoring

| Age Group | Development Stage | Leo Scoring Adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| **U10–U12** | Basic positioning; can't yet read opposition pre-pass | Reward simple box-to-box runs; lower expectations for defensive anticipation; mistakes are developmental |
| **U14–U16** | Starting to read space and timing of runs; defensive reading still inconsistent | Expect some half-space awareness; penalty for being out of position; reward anticipatory interceptions |
| **U18–College** | Full positional responsibility; reading game is expected; decision-making under pressure is critical | High penalties for lack of discipline; reward for playing with tempo; punish poor decision-making (turnover, panic pass) |
| **Professional** | Every action is purposeful; positioning is autonomous; communication is constant | Elite scoring: reward for opening passing lanes, tempo control, transition speed; harsh penalties for positional lapses |

## Elite CM Types & Their Radar Profiles

**1. The Maestro Conductor** (Rodri, Pedri, Thiago)
- Radar emphasis: Awareness, reading the game, decision-making
- Radar weakness: Box-to-box runs (plays less as an attacker, more as a controller)
- Scoring approach: High marks for positioning and anticipation; lower marks for attacking contributions

**2. The Box-to-Box Battler** (Javi Martínez, N'Golo Kanté, Mats Weigl)
- Radar emphasis: Positioning, transition speed, tackle/recovery
- Radar weakness: Passing creativity (prefers safe passes to risky through-balls)
- Scoring approach: High marks for transition dominance; moderate marks for creative passing

**3. The Creative Progressor** (Bruno Fernandes, Philippe Coutinho, Kai Havertz)
- Radar emphasis: Passing accuracy, ball progression, decision-making
- Radar weakness: Defensive discipline (often caught out positionally)
- Scoring approach: High marks for chance creation; penalties for defensive lapses

**4. The Press-Resistant Pivot** (Jorginho, Xeka)
- Radar emphasis: Composure under pressure, possession retention, reading the press
- Radar weakness: Transition speed (prefers slower, recycled possession)
- Scoring approach: High marks for possession quality; penalties for slow play

**5. The Attacking Midfielder Playing CM** (ESR-as-CM, Foden-as-CM in some systems)
- Radar emphasis: Dribbling, passing accuracy, decision-making
- Radar weakness: Positional discipline (roams too much)
- Scoring approach: High marks for creativity and direct play; penalties for bunching up teammates

## Coaching Cues & Training Keywords

**Positioning:**
- "Stay in the half-space—not too wide, not too central"
- "You're the bridge between defense and attack—be available always"
- "Position yourself so the ball-carrier has a passing option behind them"

**Timing:**
- "Run after the ball-carrier touches the ball, not before"
- "Delay your forward run until you see space opening"
- "Get back to your defensive position before the opposition counter"

**Passing:**
- "Pass to their outside foot—give them forward options"
- "When pressed, keep the ball and find a simple passing option"
- "Look for the forward pass first; sideways pass is a second option"

**Defensive Awareness:**
- "Scan the opposition movement before the ball is played"
- "Read the striker's position; if they're isolated, step forward"
- "Transition mentality: win the ball, look forward"

## Training Exercises for CM Development

### Exercise 1: Half-Space Circulation Drill
**Objective:** Develop half-space positioning and pass selection

**Setup:**
- 12x20 yard area (or larger, depending on group size)
- 5v2 (5 attackers, 2 defenders)
- Attackers must make 10 consecutive passes
- Attackers must use half-space positioning (at least 3 passes in the half-space)

**Progression:**
- Increase pressure (3v5, then 4v5)
- Add a target (must reach the "striker" zone at the end)
- Remove a defender (force CMs to anticipate and intercept)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- How many touches in the half-space? (Quality positioning = more opportunities)
- How many forward passes vs. sideways? (Decision-making quality)

---

### Exercise 2: Box-to-Box Timing Runs
**Objective:** Develop run timing and penalty area awareness

**Setup:**
- 40x30 yard area (half a pitch, width-wise)
- 3 CMs, 2 fullbacks, 1 striker, 1 CB
- Rotate positions
- Fullback passes to CM; CM progresses and looks for a striker's run or a box-to-box timing

**Progression:**
- Add defenders (2 opposition midfielders pressure the CM)
- Add a goalkeeper (finish chance)
- Add offside line (develops run timing)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- Are runs timed correctly? (After the ball-carrier commits)
- Do runs happen in dangerous areas (penalty box, attacking third)?
- Recovery time to defensive position?

---

### Exercise 3: Transition Reading & Press/Cover Decision
**Objective:** Develop defensive anticipation and decision-making

**Setup:**
- 50x40 yard area (two boxes, one as defensive zone, one as attacking zone)
- 3v3 (CM, fullback, CB vs. striker, CM, fullback)
- Coach plays a pass to one team; that team attacks; the defending team must decide who presses and who covers
- Attacking team must score in 10 seconds

**Progression:**
- Reduce time pressure (8 seconds, 5 seconds)
- Increase field size (more space = more decisions needed)
- Add a second striker (2v3 attacking situations)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- Decision accuracy (did the CM press when they should?)
- Recovery speed (how quickly can the team reorganize after a mistake?)
- Interception rate (how many times does the CM read the pass before it's made?)

---

### Exercise 4: Dribble-to-Release Drill
**Objective:** Develop progressive dribbling with a purpose (not end-goal dribbling)

**Setup:**
- 30x20 yard area
- CMs dribble toward a cone 10 yards away
- At the cone, they must release a pass to a target 5 yards away (timing the pass so the target can continue forward)
- Increase pressure (defender chasing them; they must dribble-and-pass under pressure)

**Progression:**
- Add more targets (pass to one of three options)
- Add defenders who can intercept passes
- 2v2 (two CMs dribbling and passing, two defenders trying to intercept)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- Does the CM release the pass before the dribble ends? (No dead-end dribbles)
- Is the pass weighted correctly? (Target can continue forward)

---

### Exercise 5: Receiving & First Touch Under Pressure
**Objective:** Develop receiving quality when under medium-high pressure

**Setup:**
- Three zones: build-up zone (12x12), transition zone (12x12), attacking zone (12x12)
- 2v2 in each zone, ball rotates through all three zones
- CM must receive under increasing pressure and find a passing option

**Progression:**
- Reduce space (9x9 zones instead of 12x12)
- Increase pressure speed (defenders rush immediately upon receiving)
- Require forward passes (no backward/sideways only)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- First-touch quality under pressure (heavy touch = good; rushed touch = bad)
- Pass selection (forward vs. backward)
- Turnover rate

---

### Exercise 6: Diagonal Pass & Switch Play
**Objective:** Develop switching play from one flank to the other

**Setup:**
- Full field width, 40 yards of length (from midfield line to attacking third)
- 5v3 (RB, CM, LW attacking; CB, CDM, opposition CM defending)
- RB passes to CM; CM receives and must **switch play** with a diagonal pass to LW on the opposite flank
- Progression: Add pressure (opposition CM presses immediately)

**Progression:**
- Increase distance (switch from midfield to attacking flank)
- Reduce time (5 seconds to execute pass)
- Add a defender on the receiving flank (LW must first touch and turn)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- Diagonal pass accuracy (must be weighted and curved correctly)
- Does the switch create space for the LW to attack?
- First-touch quality of the LW (did the CM's pass facilitate forward play?)

---

### Exercise 7: Positional Discipline & Shape Maintenance
**Objective:** Develop defensive positioning and awareness without the ball

**Setup:**
- 60x40 yard area (half the field)
- 3v3 (3 attackers, 3 defenders including a CM)
- Attackers pass the ball around; defenders must maintain shape and stay in their zones
- Attackers can only score if they pass from a position outside the "CM zone" (forcing the CM to be positioned correctly to prevent them)

**Progression:**
- Increase attackers (4v3, forcing the CM to cover more space)
- Add pressure cues (if a defender presses, the CM must shift to cover the gap)
- Real-time scoring (award points for good positioning, deduct for being out of zone)

**Leo Scoring Watch:**
- Is the CM staying in their "zone" or roaming?
- When shape is broken (fullback presses forward), does the CM shift to cover?
- Is the CM providing a passing option behind the ball-carrier?
