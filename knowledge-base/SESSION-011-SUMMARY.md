## Key Differentiation Notes for Leo Scoring

### ST vs. CDM: How Scoring Weights Differ

| Dimension | ST Weight | CDM Weight |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Shooting (Execution) | **HIGHEST** | Minimal |
| First Touch (Execution) | **HIGHEST** | High |
| Distribution (Execution) | Moderate | **HIGHEST** |
| Positioning (Probability) | High | **HIGHEST** |
| Awareness (Probability) | Moderate-High | **HIGHEST** |
| Decision Making (Probability) | **Highest** | High |
| Tackling (Execution) | Minimal | Moderate |

### Critical Scoring Notes

- **ST errors in the final third (bad touch, poor finish) are heavily penalized** — the position demands technical execution in high-consequence zones
- **CDM errors in central defensive areas (ball lost under press, wrong-time press) are heavily penalized** — these directly cause goal-scoring opportunities for the opposition
- **CDM's best actions are often invisible** — Leo must evaluate positioning and cover shadows proactively, not just when the CDM has the ball
- **ST's best actions are often off-ball** — run quality, movement patterns, and transition exploits must be scored even when the pass never arrives
