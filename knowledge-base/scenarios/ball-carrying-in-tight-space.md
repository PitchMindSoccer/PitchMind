# Scenario: Ball Carrying in Tight Space

## What it is
A player receives the ball in a congested area (multiple defenders nearby, <3 meters from nearest opponent) and must decide whether to:
1. Carry the ball and attempt to move into space or past defenders
2. Play a quick pass to escape pressure
3. Play a safe backwards/sideways pass

Elite players can execute any of these; poor decision-makers often choose the wrong action.

## Why it matters (player development angle)
This is a critical match scenario because it tests:
- **Press resistance**: Can the player handle pressure without panicking or losing the ball?
- **Spatial awareness**: Are there pockets of space the player can access through carrying?
- **Risk assessment**: Is the potential reward of a forward carry worth the risk of losing possession?
- **Technical execution**: Can they complete a carry in tight space without being dispossessed?
- **Decision speed**: Do they make the choice immediately or does hesitation allow defenders to compress further?

Young players often make poor choices here—either attempting unnecessary dribbles that lose possession or playing backward passes that lose attacking momentum.

## What GOOD looks like
**Press-resistant carrier (example: Sávio, Manchester City)**
- Receives in tight space with defender nearby
- Uses body and dribbling to move into 2+ meters of space while carrying
- Maintains ball close while in tight space; extends it to strike zone only when in looser area
- Over 85% success rate on carries even from tight space positions
- OBV (On-Ball Value) is positive even in tight space—carries create value

**Safe decision-maker (example: Callum Hudson-Odoi, against tight space)**
- Receives in tight space with defender in front blocking forward option
- Recognizes the press and plays pass to safer teammate (backward/lateral)
- Pass completion rate is very high because selection is low-risk
- Loss of attacking momentum is acceptable because possession is retained

**Creative decision (example: players who dribble past defenders)**
- Receives in tight space one-on-one with defender
- Evaluates defender's body position and gaps
- Executes dribble move (quick feet, body feint) to evade
- Emerges into space with clear passing/shooting options

**Risk/reward balance (example: elite midfielders)**
- Attempt carries in tight space only when success probability is high OR when reward (direct shot/key pass) justifies risk
- Have multiple options if carry is blocked (quick pass, shielding, back-heel)
- Maintain composure—not panicking into bad decisions

## What POOR looks like
- **Panicked carry**: Receiving under pressure and immediately trying to dribble without assessment
- **Too many touches in tight space**: Taking 3+ touches when 1-2 would escape, allowing more pressure to arrive
- **Isolation carry**: Attempting to carry past multiple defenders without nearby passing options if dispossessed
- **Ignoring pressure**: Not acknowledging nearest defender and running straight into them
- **Slow decision**: Taking 1+ second to decide, at which point space has compressed further
- **Forced pass**: Playing an inaccurate quick pass because of pressure, leading to interception
- **Backward dependency**: Always playing backward when under pressure, never attempting forward actions in tight space
- **Technical failure**: Dispossessing yourself with poor first touch or sloppy dribbling in tight area

## Scenario breakdown
**Situation A: Midfielder receives in tight space, defender in front (blocking forward), wide space available to side**
- GOOD: Quick lateral pass to winger, spreading play
- POOR: Forcing pass through tight central space, leading to interception
- GOOD (risky): Quick feet dribble to move around defender, creating passing angle

**Situation B: Midfielder receives in tight space in final third, shooter nearby**
- GOOD (if press-resistant): Carrying forward 1-2 meters to create shot for teammate
- GOOD (if accurate): Quick pass to shooter even though pass is tight
- POOR: Holding ball too long, allowing defenders to swarm and steal

**Situation C: Midfielder receives in tight space deep in own half, attackers up field**
- GOOD: Safety pass, reset, then build again from depth (no risk)
- RISKY but OK: One-touch pass to release forward runner (long and direct)
- POOR: Attempting to dribble out of tight space when you're outnumbered

## Key coaching points
- **Teach reading defenders**: When receiving, quickly assess:
  - How many defenders are near (1, 2, 3+)?
  - Where is space (forward, sideways, back)?
  - Are there teammates nearby to receive?
- **Pressure decision tree**:
  - Immediate pressure (defender <1 meter): Pass quickly or shield
  - Medium pressure (1-2 meters): Can consider carrying if skilled and space available
  - Loose pressure (2+ meters): Have time to assess and execute
- **Carrying in tight space requires**:
  - Small, quick touches (not heavy first touch)
  - Body position protecting ball (shield from defender)
  - Knowledge of next action before receiving
  - Above-average press resistance
- **Quality over quantity**: Not all players should attempt carries in tight space. Know your player's press-resistance ability
- **Video analysis**: Film tight-space decisions and categorize as:
  - Good decision (correct action chosen)
  - Right action, poor execution (right choice, botched technique)
  - Wrong decision (bad choice even if executed well)
  - Poor execution (right choice, poor technique)
- **Data tracking**: 
  - OBV in tight space (OBV when carry starts <3m from defender)
  - Dispossession ratio in tight space
  - Pass completion from tight space (quick passes out of pressure)
  - Carry success from tight space (carry end location with space advantage)

## Match context adjustments
- **Score/phase**: If losing, risk-taking in tight space is acceptable. If winning, pass out and maintain possession
- **Opponent quality**: Against elite pressers, tighter space is norm—must be press-resistant. Against low-pressing teams, space is looser
- **Position**: 
  - Center-backs: Safety over risk in own half
  - Midfielders: Can take calculated risks
  - Forwards: Higher risk tolerance in final third
- **Teammate positioning**: If no nearby outlet, carrying in tight space is higher risk (less safe-pass option)
