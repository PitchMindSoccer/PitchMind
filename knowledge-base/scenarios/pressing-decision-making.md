# Scenario: Pressing Decision-Making

## What It Is
Pressing decision is the choice to press (apply pressure to ball carrier) vs. hold position (wait for better opportunity). Pressing is defensive aggression; holding is patience. Elite defenders know WHEN to press (high-probability situations) vs. when to hold (risky situations). This scenario trains players to make correct pressing decisions in game-realistic situations.

## Why It Matters
Poor pressing decisions lead to:
- **Over-pressing**: Pressing without cover, allowing dangerous counter-attacks, conceding goals
- **Under-pressing**: Not pressing when should, allowing opposition to build up play, conceding shots
- **Ineffective pressing**: Pressing the wrong player, pressing without support, wasted effort

Elite teams have pressing triggers—specific situations where pressing is HIGH-PROBABILITY. Young players must learn these triggers and understand when to press vs. hold.

## Universal Pressing Decision Framework

Before pressing, defender should ask:

```
1. DO I HAVE COVER BEHIND ME?
   YES (teammate covering space I'll vacate) → Can press
   NO → Risky to press

2. IS THE BALL CARRIER IN A DANGEROUS POSITION?
   YES (close to goal, direct threat) → Should press
   NO (deep, low threat) → Maybe hold

3. ARE MY TEAMMATES NEARBY TO SUPPORT?
   YES (nearby defenders can shift) → Press is safer
   NO (isolated, no support) → High risk to press

4. WHAT'S MY TEAM'S DEFENSIVE SYSTEM?
   High press (proactive aggression) → Press more frequently
   Low block (defensive, reactive) → Press only in clear situations
   Hybrid/selective → Press based on ball location

5. HOW MUCH TIME/SPACE DOES OPPONENT HAVE?
   Limited time/space (crowded, pressure nearby) → Press is effective
   Abundant time/space (open, no pressure) → Press might be beaten
```

## Scenario 1: Opposition Center-Back in Possession (Deep)

### Situation
- Opposition center-back has just received ball, deep in their half (40 yards from your goal)
- Your striker is 2 meters away (could press)
- Your midfielders are 5 meters away (could support)
- Your defense line is compact and deep (full defensive shape)
- Opposition has numerical advantage in midfield (3 players for their transition)

### Pressing Decision
**HOLD POSITION** (Don't press)

Why not press?
1. **Ball carrier is deep** (low immediate threat)
2. **Opposition has midfield advantage** (3v2 in midfield if your midfielder leaves)
3. **No clear cover**: Your midfielder would leave space if they commit
4. **Result**: Pressing would create counter-attack opportunity for opposition
5. **Better approach**: Let opposition move the ball, then press when they advance to midfield

### Consequence of Poor Decision (Over-Pressing)
If striker presses immediately:
1. Striker closes down center-back (high-pressure situation)
2. Center-back passes to midfielder (easy because of space)
3. Midfielder has time/space (strike partner has not compressed)
4. Opposition counter-attack is dangerous (striker is out of position)
5. Result: Goal-scoring opportunity conceded

### Good Decision Making
1. Striker holds position (doesn't press immediately)
2. Opposition builds up play (center-back to midfielder to fullback)
3. **At some point, pressing trigger occurs** (e.g., opposition reaches final third, or fullback receives in deep position)
4. Then collective press is applied with cover
5. Result: Possession regained in more dangerous zone

### Coaching Points
1. **Patience in defense**: Young players press too aggressively. Teach patience.
2. **Press timing**: Pressing is effective when opposition is in specific zones (attacking third, final third).
3. **Cover principle**: Before pressing, ensure teammate covers the space you vacate.
4. **Team system**: High-press teams press more. Low-block teams press less. Know your system.

## Scenario 2: Opposition Fullback Receiving on Wing (Attacking Third)

### Situation
- Opposition fullback receives ball on right wing, 25 yards from your goal (attacking third)
- Your winger is 2 meters away (could press)
- Your fullback is 3 meters away (can support)
- Your center-back is 8 meters away (can cover)
- Opposition has created overload (fullback + winger + midfielder, 3v2)

### Pressing Decision
**PRESS IMMEDIATELY** (Close down fullback)

Why press?
1. **Ball carrier is in dangerous zone** (attacking third, close to goal)
2. **Numerical disadvantage** (3v2), but pressing with cover is best defense
3. **Fullback is not primary threat** (winger is more dangerous), so fullback receiving is okay moment to press
4. **Cover available**: Fullback and center-back can organize cover
5. **Result**: Pressing reduces time/space, forces poor decision or turnover

### Consequence of Not Pressing (Under-Pressing)
If you hold position:
1. Fullback has space and time
2. Fullback can cross immediately (dangerous)
3. Winger is free to run (overload situation)
4. Result: High-quality crossing opportunity

### Good Pressing Action
1. Winger presses fullback immediately (close distance to 1 meter)
2. Fullback is rushed (must decide quickly)
3. Fullback either:
   - Plays poor cross (because of pressure)
   - Passes back (loses attacking opportunity)
   - Loses ball (turnover)
4. Your team regains possession or prevents quality cross
5. Result: Pressing was effective

### Coaching Points
1. **Pressing triggers**: Opposition in attacking third is typical pressing trigger
2. **Collective shape**: When one defender presses, others adjust immediately (cover and balance)
3. **Patience + aggression**: Early patience (deep zone), then aggression (attacking zone)
4. **Risk management**: Pressing in attacking zone is lower-risk than pressing deep zone

## Scenario 3: Opposition Midfielder in Midfield (Plenty of Space)

### Situation
- Opposition midfielder receives ball in central midfield (30-40 yards from goal)
- Large space around midfielder (other players are not nearby)
- Your midfielder is 3 meters away (could press)
- Your defensive line is deep
- Opposition has more space than pressure (not urgent situation)

### Pressing Decision
**CONDITIONAL: Hold position** (Unless specific trigger applies)

Why hold?
1. **Midfielder has space** (pressing would require sprinting, allowing space to open)
2. **No immediate threat** (midfield is not final third, distance is far)
3. **Better to compact**: Defend deeper, let opposition advance, then apply pressure in more controlled way
4. **Risk**: Pressing in space might be beaten (midfielder has time to turn and pass)
5. **Result**: Holding compact shape is more effective

### Exception: Specific Trigger (Press Anyway)
If team is using **high-press system** (proactive, aggressive approach):
- Press more aggressively throughout pitch
- Midfielder presses immediately
- Accept higher risk for potential turnovers
- Result: Different system (high-press) requires different triggers

### Good Decision (Hold Position)
1. Your midfielder doesn't press (stays compact)
2. Opposition midfielder advances or passes
3. Opposition reaches more dangerous zone
4. **Collective press is applied when opposition is in final third** (better-controlled pressing)
5. Result: Possession regained in more dangerous zone for opposition

### Coaching Points
1. **System dependency**: Pressing decisions depend on team system (high press vs. low block)
2. **Spacial awareness**: If space is large, pressing might be ineffective
3. **Compact defending**: Holding shape is often better than pressing without cover
4. **Trigger recognition**: Identify when pressing is appropriate for your system

## Scenario 4: Opposition Fullback Receiving, Poor Support (Isolated)

### Situation
- Opposition fullback receives ball on wing (attacking third)
- Fullback is somewhat isolated (nearest midfielder is 5 meters away)
- Your fullback is 2 meters away (can press)
- Your center-back is 6 meters away (can provide cover)
- Opposition has only 2 players in this area (not overloaded)

### Pressing Decision
**PRESS IMMEDIATELY** (High-probability situation)

Why press?
1. **Fullback is isolated** (not well-supported)
2. **Your cover is available** (center-back nearby, can cover)
3. **Ball is in attacking zone** (dangerous zone, should press)
4. **Numerical balance**: 2v2 or 3v2 in the area (pressing is not reckless)
5. **Result**: Pressing isolated fullback has high probability of regaining possession

### Consequence of Not Pressing (Poor Decision)
If you hold position:
1. Fullback has space and is not pressed
2. Fullback can cross or advance (dangerous)
3. Support will arrive (midfielder arrives, making situation worse)
4. Result: Opposition creates chance

### Good Pressing Action
1. Your fullback presses isolated opposition fullback immediately
2. Opposition fullback is rushed (must decide quickly)
3. Fullback either:
   - Turns and loses ball (poor first touch)
   - Plays cross into pressure (poor quality)
   - Passes back (loses attacking opportunity)
4. Your team regains possession
5. Result: Effective pressing

### Coaching Points
1. **Isolated players**: Press isolated ball carriers (low-support situations)
2. **Cover + aggression**: Pressing is safer when cover is nearby
3. **Attacking third**: Press more aggressively in attacking third
4. **Support monitoring**: Watch if support is arriving (may need to hold instead of press)

## Scenario 5: Opposition Goalkeeper Distributing, Organized Defense

### Situation
- Opposition goalkeeper has just distributed to center-back (organized play starting)
- Your team is in low-block defensive shape (compact, deep)
- Your striker is 5 meters away from opposition center-back
- Your defensive system is **low-block** (not high-press)
- Opposition has numerical advantage in midfield (not isolated)

### Pressing Decision
**HOLD POSITION** (Stay in low-block shape)

Why hold?
1. **Team system is low-block** (not high-press)
2. **Opposition is organized** (not in transition, has time to build up)
3. **Striker pressing would expose defense** (no cover behind striker)
4. **Better approach**: Let opposition advance, apply pressure in midfield or attacking third
5. **Result**: Holding shape is appropriate for low-block system

### System Contrast (What Would Happen in High-Press)
If team is using **high-press system**:
- Striker WOULD press immediately
- Midfield would shift up
- Defense would step up
- Result: Collective high-press activation
- Different decision because different system

### Good Low-Block Defense
1. Striker holds position (doesn't press)
2. Midfielders are compact, ready to intercept
3. Defense line is organized, prepared for opposition advancement
4. **At some point, opposition advances to dangerous zone**
5. Collective press is applied (not individually, but collectively)
6. Result: Controlled defending, good shape

### Coaching Points
1. **System awareness**: Know your team's system (high-press or low-block)
2. **Trigger recognition by system**: High-press presses early. Low-block presses later (in attacking zones).
3. **Collective vs. individual**: Don't press individually in low-block (no cover). Press collectively.
4. **Discipline**: Young players in low-block want to press immediately. Teach patience.

## Pressing Decision Framework by System

### High-Press Teams
**Philosophy**: Pressure opposition early, regain possession in their half

| Situation | Decision |
|-----------|----------|
| Opposition GK distributing | PRESS (or at least pressure) |
| Opposition center-back in possession | PRESS (esp if striker is nearby) |
| Opposition in midfield building | PRESS (depending on position) |
| Opposition in attacking third | ALWAYS PRESS |

**Triggers**: Time/location-based. Press in certain zones, not in others.

### Low-Block Teams
**Philosophy**: Defend deep, wait for opposition mistakes, press selectively

| Situation | Decision |
|-----------|----------|
| Opposition GK distributing | HOLD (let them build up) |
| Opposition center-back in possession | HOLD (unless very specific trigger) |
| Opposition in midfield | HOLD (unless isolated) |
| Opposition in attacking third | PRESS (esp if isolated or numerical advantage) |

**Triggers**: Isolation-based or zone-based. Press when opposition is isolated or in dangerous zone.

### Hybrid Systems
**Philosophy**: Mix high-press and low-block based on situation

| Situation | Decision |
|-----------|----------|
| Opposition GK distributing | Depends on opponent, score, phase |
| Opposition center-back in possession | Depends on fullback/midfielder support |
| Opposition in midfield | Press if isolated, hold if supported |
| Opposition in attacking third | PRESS (always) |

**Triggers**: Flexible. Based on opposition positioning, team situation, match phase.

## Pressing Decision Training

### Training Drill 1: Video Analysis
- Show video clips of pressing decisions
- Pause before pressing action, ask: "Press or hold? Why?"
- Discuss cover, triggers, system
- Show actual outcome, analyze decision quality

### Training Drill 2: Pressure Trigger Recognition
- Coach calls out situations: "Opposition fullback on wing, no support, attacking third. Press or hold?"
- Players respond quickly (press, hold, conditional)
- Discuss reasoning
- Build pattern recognition

### Training Drill 3: Small-Sided Pressing Drills
- 4v4 or 5v5, coach designates zones (high-press in final third, low-block in own half)
- Players learn triggers by experience
- Coach rewards good pressing (turnover), punishes poor pressing (counter-attack)

### Training Drill 4: System-Specific Pressing
- Build team's pressing system
- Teach collective pressing triggers
- Practice coordinated pressing (all players press together, not individually)
- Emphasize cover and balance

## Key Principles to Reinforce

1. **System matters**: High-press teams press more. Low-block teams press less.
2. **Triggers are learned**: Identify situations where pressing is high-probability (isolated players, attacking zones, etc.)
3. **Cover is essential**: Press only if cover is available. Pressing without cover = counter-attack.
4. **Collective not individual**: Pressing is team action, not solo player action.
5. **Patience + aggression**: Patient in low-risk zones, aggressive in high-risk zones.
6. **Zone management**: Different zones have different pressing rules.
7. **Composure**: Even good pressing decision requires proper execution (tight marking, pressure).

## Pressing vs. Holding by Field Zone

| Zone | Pressing Rule | Reasoning |
|------|---|---|
| **Own half** (defending) | HOLD (unless isolated) | Protect goal, prevent counter-attack |
| **Midfield** | CONDITIONAL (depends on isolation/support) | Prevent midfield control |
| **Attacking third** | PRESS (aggressive) | Regain possession in dangerous zone |
| **Penalty area** | PRESS (always) | Prevent shots on goal |

## Common Pressing Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Over-Pressing Without Cover
**Situation**: Striker presses opposition center-back without defensive cover
**Result**: Center-back passes around, counter-attack creates goal-scoring chance
**Fix**: Teach defensive organization. Press only with cover available.

### Mistake 2: Under-Pressing Isolated Players
**Situation**: Opposition fullback is isolated on wing, not pressed
**Result**: Fullback has space and time, delivers dangerous cross
**Fix**: Teach trigger recognition. Isolated players should be pressed.

### Mistake 3: Late Pressing (Reacting)
**Situation**: Defender waits for opposition pass, then presses
**Result**: Opposition has already released pass, pressing is ineffective
**Fix**: Anticipatory pressing. Press before opponent passes (not after).

### Mistake 4: Individual Pressing (No Collective Shape)
**Situation**: One player presses, others don't adjust
**Result**: Gaps open, opposition counter-attacks
**Fix**: Teach collective pressing. When one presses, others adjust immediately.
