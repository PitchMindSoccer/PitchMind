# Scenario: Decision-Making Under Pressure

## What it is
When a player receives the ball with an opponent approaching (pressing), they have roughly 1-2 seconds to decide their next action. Under pressure, decision-making quality often drops because the player is focused on the immediate threat (the pressing defender) rather than reading the full field. This scenario tests:

1. **Pressure recognition** (How quickly can I identify I'm being pressed?)
2. **Decision speed** (Can I make the best choice in <1 second?)
3. **Technical execution** (Can I execute the decision accurately while stressed?)
4. **Option hierarchy** (What's the best option? If that's not available, what's next?)

## Why it matters (player development angle)
In modern soccer, high pressing means most players spend 30-40% of possession under some level of pressure. Players who make good decisions under pressure are worth significantly more because they:
- Retain possession when others would lose it
- Find the dangerous pass when others would play safe
- Escape pressure through movement or quick action instead of panicking
- Build confidence—teammates trust them to keep possession

Young players often freeze or panic under pressure, leading to poor decisions (turnover, backward pass, unnecessary foul).

## What GOOD looks like

**Recognizing pressure and responding calmly:**
- Player receives ball and immediately scans to locate nearest defender
- Takes touch away from pressure (lateral or backward)
- Maintains composure (not rushed body language)
- Executes best available option accurately

**Quick decision hierarchy under pressure:**
1. **First option**: Forward pass to teammate in space/advantageous position (if available, do this)
2. **Second option**: Lateral pass to teammate, spreading play and escaping immediate pressure
3. **Third option**: Backward pass to a supporting teammate (lowest risk, loses some momentum)
4. **Fourth option**: Carry the ball if you have space to move into and are press-resistant
5. **Last resort**: Shield the ball and reset (wait for more support)

**Example: Elite midfielder (Rodri, Jorginho) under pressure**
- Receives with defender approaching
- Takes touch away from pressure (maintains composure)
- Identifies best passing option (usually forward) within 1 second
- Executes pass with accuracy (>85% completion even under pressure)
- Ball is played at correct pace/weight (not misplaced)

**Example: Press-resistant winger (Sávio, Doku) under pressure**
- Recognizes pressure but assesses if they can carry into space
- Moves touch into available space while shielding from defender
- If space available, carries and creates advantage
- If press is effective, plays accurate quick pass to escape

## What POOR looks like
- **Delayed recognition**: Doesn't notice pressure until defender is on top of them
- **Panicked first touch**: Heavy touch under pressure, immediately lose possession
- **Forced pass**: Playing an inaccurate pass because of pressure, intercepted
- **Hesitation**: Takes 2+ seconds to decide, opposition has compressed further
- **Back-pass dependency**: Always playing backward under pressure, losing attacking momentum
- **Isolation decision**: Trying to dribble out when surrounded, losing ball
- **Technical failure**: Shaky execution of intended action because they're stressed
- **No next option**: Playing their only option when it's closed, result in bad decision

## Decision-making frameworks under pressure

### Framework A: Time-Based Response
**<0.5 seconds**: Immediate carry if clear space, or immediate short pass
**0.5-1.0 seconds**: More passing options visible, can identify best option
**1.0-2.0 seconds**: Multiple options available, can consider risk/reward
**>2 seconds**: Typically means pressure failed or you've shifted play; more time available

### Framework B: Space-Based Response
**Tight space (<2m to defender)**: 
- Defend: Safety pass (sideways/backward)
- Attack: Quick carry with close touch, or rapid pass
- Don't: Dribble with loose touch; forces will dispossess

**Medium space (2-4m to defender)**:
- Defend: Quick pass to support
- Attack: Can carry 1-2 meters into space, or pass forward
- Don't: Hesitate; decision must be made quickly

**Loose space (4+ m to defender)**:
- Defend: Can afford more deliberate choice
- Attack: Multiple options available; look for progressive pass
- Can: Take extra touch to set up perfect pass

### Framework C: Numbers-Based Response (Numerical Situations)
**Outnumbered (more defenders than attackers nearby)**:
- Safety first: Play backward/sideways, wait for support
- Don't: Try to create magic pass or dribble through multiple opponents

**Matched numbers (equal defenders/attackers)**:
- Look for movement and opportunity
- Quick pass to teammate in slightly better position
- Can risk a forward pass if timing is right

**Overloaded (more attackers than defenders)**:
- Progressive/dangerous pass is the goal
- Forward pass or carry forward is high-reward
- Can risk slightly more

## Scenario breakdown

**Scenario A: Midfielder receives with one defender pressing closely, teammates nearby**
- GOOD: Quick sideways pass to teammate 5 meters away, spreading play
- GOOD: If aware of forward option, quick forward pass between lines
- GOOD (if press-resistant): Touch away from pressure, quick assessment, then best available pass
- POOR: Forced forward pass through pressure, intercepted
- POOR: Panic and play backward, losing momentum

**Scenario B: Defender receives under pressure from forward, center-back nearby as support**
- GOOD: Safety pass to center-back, resetting
- GOOD: Quick sideways pass to fullback if available
- POOR: Trying to dribble around forward; risky
- POOR: Long ball forward without accuracy; turnover likely

**Scenario C: Fullback receiving on wing under pressure from winger**
- GOOD: Quick infield pass to midfielder, spreading play
- GOOD: Carry forward if able (fullback at space), or back-pass to center-back
- POOR: Immediate cross under pressure (likely inaccurate)
- POOR: Standing still; letting pressure compress further

**Scenario D: Forward receiving back-to-goal under pressure**
- GOOD: Quick flick pass to midfielder, quick turn with space available
- GOOD: Shield ball and hold, wait for teammate to arrive in support
- POOR: Trying to turn against centerback without space; dispossession likely
- POOR: Blind pass without looking; turnover

## Key coaching points

### Pressure Decision Training
- **Under-pressure drills**: 1v1 receiving with passive→active pressure
- **Multiple-choice drills**: Create scenarios with 2-3 passing options; which is best under pressure?
- **Pressure + fatigue**: Add running before receiving (simulate fatigue); test decision quality when tired
- **Game-speed drills**: Realistic game tempo, not slow-motion coaching

### Teaching Decision Speed
- **Scanning before receiving**: Player looks at field BEFORE ball arrives; knows options already
- **Decision tree**: "If this... then this" (if tight space → pass; if open space → carry)
- **Confidence building**: Successful decisions under pressure build confidence; practice winning scenarios
- **Pressure acceptance**: Some pressure is normal and expected; not every situation is an emergency

### Quality Metrics Under Pressure
- **Completion rate under pressure**: % of passes completed when being pressed (should be >80%)
- **Forward pass frequency under pressure**: % of under-pressure decisions that are forward vs. backward
- **Carry success under pressure**: % of carries that result in positive progression or escape
- **Decision speed**: Time from receiving to executing action (should be <1.5 seconds for elite)
- **Pressure-induced error rate**: Turnovers caused by poor under-pressure decisions

### Position-Specific Adjustments
- **Center-back**: Comfort under pressure is critical; safety is high priority
- **Midfielder**: Should combine safety with forward-looking decisions
- **Winger**: More freedom to dribble under pressure if press-resistant
- **Forward**: Often isolated under pressure; must decide quickly between hold-up and passing

### Video Analysis Framework
For each under-pressure situation, code as:
1. **Excellent**: Right decision, executed well, positive outcome
2. **Good**: Right decision, executed well, neutral or positive outcome
3. **Okay**: Acceptable decision/execution, possible better option existed
4. **Poor**: Wrong decision or poor execution; neutral or negative outcome
5. **Turnover**: Loss of possession due to under-pressure failure

Track trends: Is this player improving under pressure? Which scenarios are they struggling with?

### Psychological Component
- **Manage stress**: Breathing, focus, self-talk during pressure
- **Positive experience**: Successful pressure moments build confidence
- **Mistake management**: Poor under-pressure decision shouldn't destroy confidence for next 10 minutes
- **Teamwork**: Knowing teammates have your back (covering space if you make risky decision) builds confidence

## Match preparation
- Review opposition pressing triggers and patterns before match
- Identify which players will likely be pressed and which will have space
- Practice against similar pressure styles in the week before match
- Communicate: "If you're pressed, X is your out pass" (clear, pregame understanding)
