## Completed Documents

### 1. STRIKER ARCHETYPES (ST-archetype-profiles.md)
**4 Archetypes | 62 KB**

#### 1.1 Link-up Striker
The anti-poacher facilitator. Back-to-goal craftsman who connects play through hold-up, layoffs, and combination. Goals are a byproduct.
- **Elite examples:** Roberto Firmino, Karim Benzema
- **Key trait:** Pass-first mentality, shielding mastery, one-touch distributions
- **Weakness:** Fewer goals, less explosive
- **Best pairing:** Complete Striker, Pressing Mid

#### 1.2 Channel Runner / Poacher
Pure movement and clinical finishing. Exploits space relentlessly — diagonal runs in behind, late arrivals into the box, one-touch finishing.
- **Elite examples:** Kylian Mbappé, Jamie Vardy
- **Key trait:** Run timing precision, directional finishing, offside trap mastery
- **Weakness:** Limited team play, vulnerable to tight marking
- **Best pairing:** Link-up Striker, Wide Forward

#### 1.3 Complete Striker
The total forward. Can do everything — hold up, run channels, shoot with both feet, win headers, press from front. Modern "9."
- **Elite examples:** Robert Lewandowski, Harry Kane, Didier Drogba
- **Key trait:** Adaptive decision-making, movement versatility, technical completeness
- **Weakness:** System dependency, high-pressure expectations
- **Best pairing:** Link-up Striker, Ball-Playing CM

#### 1.4 False 9
The architect wearing a striker's shirt. Drops deep to receive, creates from unusual positions, uses dribbling and vision to generate overloads.
- **Elite examples:** Lionel Messi, Sergio Agüero, Francesco Totti
- **Key trait:** Deep reception, ball carrying mastery, positional disruption
- **Weakness:** Low goal tally, system dependency, pressing vulnerability
- **Best pairing:** Poacher, Complete Striker

### 2. CENTRAL MIDFIELDER ARCHETYPES (CM-archetype-profiles.md)
**4 Archetypes | 45 KB**

#### 2.1 Box-to-Box Midfielder
The engine room. Covers every blade of grass — defends one moment, arrives in box the next. Most team-first archetype.
- **Elite examples:** Steven Gerrard, İlkay Gündoğan
- **Key trait:** Phase transition intelligence, late box arrivals, tackle-and-recycle cycle
- **Weakness:** Inconsistency, fatigue vulnerability
- **Best pairing:** Regista, Creative CAM

#### 2.2 Regista / Deep Playmaker
The quarterback. Drops deep, receives from CBs, orchestrates play with vision and precision. Slowest and most intelligent CM archetype.
- **Elite examples:** Andrea Pirlo, Sergio Busquets, Xavi Hernandez
- **Key trait:** Pre-scanning habit, long switches, press escape mastery
- **Weakness:** Vulnerable to high press, lacks defensive contribution
- **Best pairing:** Box-to-Box Midfielder, Ball-Playing CB

#### 2.3 Progressive Carrier
The line-breaker. Receives in midfield, identifies gap, drives through it, releases precisely. Modern football's most valued trait.
- **Elite examples:** Kevin De Bruyne, Luka Modrić, Frenkie de Jong
- **Key trait:** Line-breaking carry, gap identification, carry-to-release pass
- **Weakness:** Turnover risk, limited playmaking range
- **Best pairing:** Regista, Creative CAM

#### 2.4 Pressing Mid / Workhorse
The defensive foundation. Covers, presses, wins balls, recycles immediately. Makes creative players around them possible.
- **Elite examples:** N'Golo Kanté, Jordan Henderson, Leon Goretzka
- **Key trait:** Tackle-and-recycle pattern, spatial cover, press-trigger response
- **Weakness:** Limited attacking output, reliance on team structure
- **Best pairing:** Creative CAM, Progressive Carrier

### 3. ATTACKING MIDFIELDER ARCHETYPES (CAM-archetype-profiles.md)
**4 Archetypes | 15 KB**

#### 3.1 Creative Playmaker (Classic 10)
The metronome. Sees passes before they exist. Operates in half-spaces, controls tempo. **Attacks become better when they touch the ball.**
- **Elite examples:** Kevin De Bruyne, Andrés Iniesta, Mesut Özil
- **Key trait:** Pre-scanning, weight-of-pass mastery, half-space positioning
- **Weakness:** Limited goal threat, vulnerable to pressing
- **Best pairing:** Goal-Threat 10, Pressing Mid

#### 3.2 Goal-Threat 10 (Trequartista)
The dangerous creator. Creates for others but always looking to shoot or dribble. Unpredictable and technical with an eye for goal.
- **Elite examples:** Paulo Dybala, Riqui Puig, Juan Mata
- **Key trait:** Tight-space dribbling, shooting from distance, creation through danger
- **Weakness:** Potential selfishness, inconsistency
- **Best pairing:** Creative Playmaker, Direct 10

#### 3.3 Press-and-Combine
The high-energy connector. Presses aggressively, wins ball high, recycles immediately. Engine that creates chaos for others to exploit.
- **Elite examples:** Bernardo Silva, Christian Eriksen, Gavi
- **Key trait:** Aggressive pressing, one-touch recycles, tempo acceleration
- **Weakness:** Limited creativity, vulnerable to slick passing
- **Best pairing:** Creative Playmaker, Ball-Playing CM

#### 3.4 Direct 10 (Carrier Playmaker)
The forward carrier. Carries ball from deep or midfield, attracts pressure, releases at last moment or shoots. Progressive and commanding.
- **Elite examples:** Bruno Fernandes, Thomas Müller, Hakim Ziyech
- **Key trait:** Progressive carries, shooting from range, release accuracy
- **Weakness:** Turnover risk, limited playmaking vision
- **Best pairing:** Creative Playmaker, Pressing Mid

### 4. DEFENSIVE MIDFIELDER ARCHETYPES (CDM-archetype-profiles.md)
**4 Archetypes | 15 KB**

#### 4.1 Pure Destroyer
The defensive shield. Physical, imposing, relentless. Wins every duel, closes every gap. Last line before defense.
- **Elite examples:** Patrick Vieira, Claude Makélélé, Roy Keane, Gennaro Gattuso
- **Key trait:** Duel dominance, zone control, aggressive pressing
- **Weakness:** Limited distribution, vulnerable to clever movement
- **Best pairing:** Ball-Playing DM

#### 4.2 Ball-Playing DM
The defensive playmaker. Defends space and initiates attacks. More technical than physical, receives from CBs and distributes with quality.
- **Elite examples:** Casemiro, Rodri, Fabinho, Fernandinho
- **Key trait:** Press escape, build-up role, versatile distribution
- **Weakness:** Caught between roles, high-press vulnerability
- **Best pairing:** Pure Destroyer, Pressing CM

#### 4.3 Libelo / Ball-Winner with Vision
The tactical genius. Reads game at meta level — not just ball but spaces, patterns, triggers. Intercepts rather than tackles. Distributes like a CM.
- **Elite examples:** Sergio Busquets, Andrea Pirlo (DM role), Toni Kroos, Jorginho
- **Key trait:** Tactical interception, pivot distribution, meta-level reading
- **Weakness:** Vulnerable to direct play, team-dependent
- **Best pairing:** Box-to-Box CM, Pressing CM

#### 4.4 High-Press Anchor
The pressing foundation. Sets press from deepest position. Reads when to push up and trap, holds line when not. Foundation of pressing.
- **Elite examples:** Scott McTominay, Kanté (in pressing systems), Youri Tielemans
- **Key trait:** Press-line management, trap coordination, spatial covering
- **Weakness:** Limited distribution, through-ball vulnerability
- **Best pairing:** Ball-Playing DM, Pressing CM

## Key Insight: The Blend Philosophy

**These are not boxes — they are gravitational centers. Every real player is a blend.**

The best players often pull toward one archetype but have elements of another:
- **Firmino:** Primarily Link-up (72%) but has Complete Striker elements (28%)
- **De Bruyne:** Primarily Progressive Carrier (65%) but has Creative Playmaker vision (35%)
- **Kanté:** Primarily Pressing Mid (70%) but has Ball-Playing DM distribution (30%)

Future PitchMind profiles should focus on:
1. Identifying the primary archetype (>50% match)
2. Identifying secondary archetypes (20-40% match)
3. Identifying unique divergent traits (player outliers from both archetypes)
4. Designing dual-path development: Path A (deepen primary), Path B (develop secondary blend)
